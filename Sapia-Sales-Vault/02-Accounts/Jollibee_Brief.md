### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** JFC runs SAP SuccessFactors Recruiting & Onboarding across a workforce base likely in the tens of thousands, screening for a network that opened the highest number of gross new stores in company history in 2025 and is guided to open a further 1,200–1,300 stores in 2026 — a sustained, high-volume frontline crew hiring load with no confirmed structured pre-hire screening or assessment layer sitting in front of SuccessFactors. (Sources: Sections 3 and 4 above.)
- **Business Pain:** JFC imposed a corporate-level headcount freeze in Q4 2025 and is actively reviewing 2026 capex and store-opening pacing after Q1 2026 attributable profit fell 38.8% on inflationary and geopolitical cost pressures, even while store network growth (and therefore frontline hiring demand) continues largely undiminished (Q1 SWS still up 10.3%). This is a textbook "do more with less" tension: Romeo Rivera's global TA function is very likely being asked to keep pace with record-breaking store expansion while corporate overhead, consultancy spend, and (implicitly) TA capacity are all under cost scrutiny. Every hour a recruiter spends manually screening CVs or phone-screening crew candidates is now a harder cost to defend internally. (Sources: [BusinessWorld — Jollibee Group reviews 2026 assumptions amid cost volatility](https://www.bworldonline.com/corporate/2026/05/13/749178/jollibee-group-reviews-2026-assumptions-amid-cost-volatility/); [Yahoo Finance — Q4 2025 earnings highlights](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/jollibee-foods-corp-jbfcf-q4-010130550.html), both pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Personal Pain:** Romeo Rivera's Global Talent Acquisition function sits underneath both a Global CHRO (Sabado) publicly staking his personal credibility on the newly-won Gallup Exceptional Workplace Award "with Distinction" (JFC's fifth consecutive engagement award, announced March 2026) and a Philippines CHRO (Angeles) responsible for the largest single market. A public, leadership-attributed commitment to "engagement built intentionally every day" raises the bar for what a candidate's first touchpoint with the brand should feel like — if frontline screening is still manual phone-tag and CV triage at store level, that is a direct contradiction risk to the external Gallup/"Choose Joy!" narrative Sabado has just put his name behind. (Source: [Jollibee Group Earns Gallup's Highest Workplace Honor — Jollibee Group](https://www.jollibeegroup.com/news/jollibee-group-earns-gallups-highest-workplace-honor-wins-engagement-award-for-fifth-year/), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Career Pain / Root Cause:** A third-party Philippine franchise-consulting source (not a Jollibee-published statistic; treat as an illustrative industry estimate, not confirmed company data) claims a Jollibee crew member requires roughly 80 hours of structured training (about ₱24,000 in wages) before reaching full productivity, and that around 40% of trained crew leave within their first year — implying a large share of that training investment is lost annually. If even directionally accurate, this sits in sharp tension with the record store-opening pace: every new store needs a fully trained crew fast, but early attrition would mean TA and store operations are perpetually re-filling the same roles rather than banking net new capacity, which is precisely the kind of invisible cost that becomes highly visible once a CFO/Group is already implementing a corporate headcount freeze and reviewing capex. Source (lower-confidence, third-party, not Jollibee-attributed): [Effective Strategies to Manage Staff Turnover in Philippine Franchises — franchisedetailsph.com](https://franchisedetailsph.com/blog/handle-staff-turnover/), pulled 14 July 2026. UNKNOWN whether Jollibee leadership has publicly acknowledged this attrition figure themselves; no primary-source confirmation found this session.
