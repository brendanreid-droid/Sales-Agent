**Date:** 14 July 2026
**Territory:** APAC (Philippines primary market)

> **Entity note and status:** Globe Telecom, Inc. is a **new lookalike candidate** surfaced this run by the Research Analyst, not previously on the vault target list, and is **not a Sapia customer**. It was provisionally scored borderline Tier A (approximately 79) on web-search-only evidence, with hiring-volume evidence flagged as the weakest of the five lookalike candidates reviewed this run. ### 1. Company Snapshot

### 4. Technology Stack Sourcing
- ATS/HRIS: **Workday, confirmed live.** Globe's careers site is hosted directly on Workday (`globe.wd3.myworkdayjobs.com/GLB_Careers`), with individually addressable requisition IDs visible in search results (e.g. Growth Manager, IT Service Manager, Office Administrator, Expert - Operations Support). A second Workday tenant (`globe.wd3.myworkdayjobs.com/Mynt`) is run for the separately-owned Mynt/GCash entity, indicating Workday is used group-wide across Globe's ecosystem, not just the parent. Source: [globe.wd3.myworkdayjobs.com/GLB_Careers](https://globe.wd3.myworkdayjobs.com/GLB_Careers), pulled 14 July 2026.
- **Assessment/screening tool: HireVue, confirmed in active, award-winning use — this is the central finding of this brief.** Globe's HR function piloted HireVue's AI-powered video interview and coding assessment (CodeVue) tools at the start of a recent hiring cycle for "digital, IT, and tech-focused groups," then rolled it out at full scale "across all groups including portfolio companies" after a successful pilot, replacing manual pre-screening. HireVue's own published customer case study on Globe states: screening-to-interview conversion improved from 23% to 38%; interview-to-offer conversion improved from 47% to 90%; time from endorsement to offer fell from 16 days to 7 days; and processing time for qualitative interview data fell by half (previously 3 to 5 days). The case study also confirms **native Workday integration** ("seamless connectivity between systems with minimal change management needs"), and separately confirms use of HireVue's Interview Scheduler. Globe describes the outcome as bringing "consistency and a more scientific approach to hiring," eliminating disparities between individual recruiters' screening methods. Sources: [HireVue — How Globe Telecom's Talent function became an established partner to the business](https://www.hirevue.com/resources/video/how-globe-telecoms-talent-became-partner), pulled 14 July 2026; [insiderph.com — Inside Globe's AI shift](https://insiderph.com/inside-globes-ai-shift-smarter-hiring-faster-workflows-better-cx), pulled 14 July 2026; [bilyonaryo.com, 30 May 2025](https://bilyonaryo.com/2025/06/01/globe-telecom-ramps-up-ai-use-to-boost-hiring-and-network-efficiency/technology/), pulled 14 July 2026.

It is a genuine competitive account already running HireVue at scale, has won two consecutive years of HireVue's own customer excellence awards (Rising Star 2024, Impact Star 2025), and has had its CEO publicly praise the AI hiring rollout. Treat this as a **displacement opportunity, not a green-field opportunity**, and expect a materially harder, longer sales cycle than a typical "no structured screening tool" account.

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** Globe's HireVue-based screening delivers genuinely strong stated metrics (23% to 38% screen-to-interview conversion, 16 to 7 days endorsement-to-offer) but is a **video-and-audio-based** AI assessment tool, which under emerging Philippine NPC guidance (Advisory 2024-04 / Advisory Opinion 2024-002, effective focus as of the 25 February 2026 briefing) now carries explicit, documented compliance obligations: mandatory human review of adverse decisions, periodic bias audits, and privacy-notice disclosure of AI use, with **accountability resting on the employer, "regardless of vendor involvement."** This is a live, current compliance workload sitting on Jiao's team that a text-only, no-video, no-audio approach (Sapia) is purpose-built to reduce. Sources: Section 3 and 4 citations above.
- **Business Pain:** Globe's own case study evidence shows HireVue's rollout was initially scoped to "digital, IT, and tech-focused groups" for hard-to-fill technical roles, then expanded company-wide "including portfolio companies" — but there is no public evidence it has been extended to, or was designed for, Globe's frontline retail (99 stores) or customer-facing volume hiring specifically. With headcount now growing again after three years of contraction (+4.6% year on year to Q1 2026) and the business reporting record FY2025 revenue and EBITDA, the TA function is very likely ramping hiring across multiple job families simultaneously while under continued "prudent cost management" pressure (explicitly named in Q1 2026 results commentary) — a tension between hiring more and spending less that is consistent across Globe's public statements. Sources: Section 1 and 3 citations above.
- **Personal Pain:** Ato Jiao's personal and professional brand is now closely tied to Globe's AI-hiring transformation story (HireVue awards, HR Icon 2026 recognition, CEO endorsement). This is a career asset, but it also means any visible bias, compliance, or candidate-experience failure tied to that specific tool carries outsized personal risk for him at exactly the moment new NPC guidance has sharpened employer accountability for AI recruitment tools "regardless of vendor involvement." UNKNOWN whether Jiao or his team has raised bias-audit or compliance concerns about HireVue publicly; no such statement was found. This is Sapia's inference from the regulatory timing and the public praise pattern, not a company admission.
- **Root Cause:** Globe has invested in and been publicly rewarded for a video-first AI screening approach concentrated in technical/digital hiring, but has comparatively thin public evidence of a structured, scaled screening solution for its ~99-store frontline retail network or broader customer-facing hiring, and now faces a newly explicit external compliance obligation (human review, bias audits, transparency) attached specifically to the kind of tool it has chosen. UNKNOWN whether this gap or obligation has been named internally by Globe; this is an evidence-based inference, not a confirmed statement.

### 9. The ONE Sourcing / Buying Signal Worth Opening On
- Globe Telecom is a genuine, currently-in-market HireVue customer with two consecutive years of vendor excellence awards and a CEO who has publicly praised the AI hiring rollout — this is not a hiring-volume pain signal in the classic Sapia playbook sense, and the honest read is that it is a **harder, longer-cycle displacement opportunity** rather than an easy in. The single most defensible, currently-relevant opening signal is regulatory, not operational: new Philippine National Privacy Commission guidance on AI in recruitment (effective focus as of the 25 February 2026 legal briefing) now places explicit, vendor-independent compliance accountability on employers using video/audio AI screening tools, exactly the category HireVue sits in and Sapia's text-only model does not. Combined with CHRO Ato Jiao's high public visibility around AI-hiring innovation (HR Icon 2026, HireVue awards), this creates a credible, risk-and-reputation-framed reason for a conversation, even though it is not a volume-crisis or candidate-experience-failure story the way Telstra's is. Sources: Sections 3, 4, 6 and 8 above.
