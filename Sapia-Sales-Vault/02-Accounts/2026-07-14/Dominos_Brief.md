# Company Brief: Domino's Pizza Enterprises Limited (ASX: DMP)
**Date:** 14 July 2026
**Territory:** APAC (ANZ primary market; entity also spans Japan, France, Germany, Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg, Taiwan, Malaysia, Singapore, Cambodia)

> **Entity note:** The target is **Domino's Pizza Enterprises Limited** (ASX:DMP), HQ Hamilton, Queensland, Australia. This is the master franchisor/operator for ANZ, Asia, Japan and parts of Europe, and a wholly separate public company from US-listed Domino's Pizza Inc (NASDAQ:DPZ), which owns the global brand and franchises DPE its territories. Do not confuse the two entities' leadership teams. This brief covers DPE (ASX:DMP) only. Source: [Domino's Pizza Enterprises — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domino's_Pizza_Enterprises), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** DPE ANZ runs an estimated 900+ stores and roughly 26,000 team members through a lightweight, non-enterprise ATS (ApplyNow) with no evidence of a structured pre-hire screening or assessment layer. Every in-store team member, delivery driver and e-bike rider role is filled through a basic application-capture tool, which typically means screening defaults to manual phone/paper review by store-level or area managers, a workforce not trained as professional recruiters. (Source for ATS: [ApplyNow AU careers site](https://dominos-australia.applynow.net.au/), pulled 14 July 2026; workforce estimate per same source.)
- **Business Pain:** DPE's own FY26 strategy is explicitly built on lifting average franchisee profitability from about A$95k to a A$130k target and cutting the unit payback period, with two-thirds of identified cost savings being passed directly to franchisees. Frontline staffing cost, vacancy and turnover directly erode exactly the store-level EBITDA the turnaround strategy is trying to protect: every unfilled delivery driver or team member shift is a direct hit to a franchisee's already-thin margin during a period when the network has just closed 205 underperforming stores and is under board and analyst scrutiny to prove unit economics work. (Source: [rogermontgomery.com — Pivoting to franchisee prosperity](https://rogermontgomery.com/dominos-pizza-enterprises-pivoting-to-franchisee-prosperity/), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Personal Pain:** With three ANZ CEOs in under two years and a new incoming Group CEO from outside the business (Andrew Gregory, ex-McDonald's, starting by August 2026), David Klages as Chief People and Culture Officer is very likely managing significant executive transition overhead on top of business-as-usual people operations across 12 markets, while also being expected to deliver on the FY26 cost-out programme and the 2030 gender diversity target (40/40/20 by 2030, part of the "30% Club"). A new incoming CEO from a highly hiring-process-mature organisation (McDonald's, which has invested heavily in structured frontline hiring technology globally) may bring outside expectations for what "modern" frontline hiring looks like. (Sources: [Bloomberg — Andrew Gregory appointment](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-10/domino-s-pizza-enterprises-names-mcdonald-s-veteran-as-new-ceo), pulled 14 July 2026; [Domino's for Good — Diversity and Inclusion](https://www.dominospizzaenterprises.com/dominos-for-good-diversity-and-inclusion), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Root Cause:** DPE is a master franchisor, not a direct employer of most frontline staff — actual hiring execution sits with individual franchisees at store level, using a shared but basic ApplyNow application portal with no group-wide structured, bias-reduced or data-led screening layer. This creates inconsistent screening quality store-to-store, no group-level visibility into candidate quality or funnel drop-off, and no shared mechanism to help franchisees hire people who stay (directly undermining the FY26 franchisee profitability target, since staff turnover and retraining cost is one of the largest controllable costs in a QSR store). UNKNOWN whether this fragmentation has been named explicitly by DPE leadership; this is Sapia's inference based on the confirmed ATS tooling gap and the franchise operating model, not a direct company statement.
