# Company Brief: FairPrice Group (NTUC FairPrice Co-operative Limited)
**Date:** 14 July 2026
**Territory:** APAC (Singapore HQ and primary operating market)

> **Entity note:** FairPrice Group is the retail arm established in 2019, bringing together NTUC FairPrice, NTUC Foodfare and Kopitiam under one group structure, operating under NTUC Enterprise Co-operative Limited. It is Singapore's largest retailer with brands spanning FairPrice supermarkets, FairPrice Finest, FairPrice Xtra (hypermarket), Unity Pharmacy and Cheers convenience stores. Source: [FairPrice Group careers page](https://www.fairpricegroup.com.sg/careers/), pulled 14 July 2026; [NTUC FairPrice — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NTUC_FairPrice), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 3. Recent News & Trigger Events (Last ~6 Months, widened given the density of the AI transformation programme)

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** FairPrice Group's roughly 13,000 employees across 570+ touchpoints are hired through x0pa.ai, a niche Singapore recruitment SaaS platform with confirmed AI-matching/CRM capability but no evidence of a structured, competency-based behavioural screening layer. With 215+ roles open continuously (Section 4) and applications also funnelled in via a third-party e2i portal before reaching the internal TA team, this points to a fragmented intake process and screening that likely defaults to manual review by TA/HR-generalist staff rather than a scaled, consistent assessment process. Sources: [x0pa.ai microsite link on FairPrice Group careers page](https://www.fairpricegroup.com.sg/careers/); [Glassdoor — ~215 open positions](https://www.glassdoor.sg/Jobs/NTUC-Fairprice-Jobs-E138849.htm); [e2i x FairPrice Group Job Portal](https://event.e2i.com.sg/view-event/fpg) — all pulled 14 July 2026.
- **Business Pain:** FairPrice names Sheng Siong as its "biggest competitor" in a highly commoditised, price-sensitive Singapore grocery market (per FairPrice's own Deputy Group CEO, reported 2023; Sheng Siong is still described in 2026 sector coverage as the clear number-two chain, having overtaken Cold Storage/CS Fresh). FairPrice is currently absorbing margin pressure directly, having frozen prices on 500+ daily essentials from June to August 2026 to stay competitive on cost of living. In a market this price-competitive and with the retailer voluntarily compressing its own margins, frontline labour cost, consistency and vacancy directly affect the thin margins the business is fighting to protect. Sources: [Mothership.sg, 2023](https://mothership.sg/2023/07/sheng-siong-fairprice-customers-win/); [FairPrice Group freezes prices — ntuc.org.sg](https://www.ntuc.org.sg/uportal/news/FairPrice-Group-freezes-prices-of-over-500-daily-essentials-to-ease-rising-cost-of-living/); [gourmetpro.co — 37 Biggest Supermarkets in Singapore in 2025](https://www.gourmetpro.co/blog/biggest-supermarkets-singapore/) — all pulled 14 July 2026.

A People leader driving a "future-ready organisational capabilities" mandate, with prior roles at more tech-mature organisations (Unilever, DFI Retail Group), is likely to feel this gap keenly. Sources: [FairPrice Group — Our Leadership](https://www.fairpricegroup.com.sg/our-story-and-purpose/our-leadership/); [techcoffeehouse.com, 23 April 2026](https://techcoffeehouse.com/2026/04/23/fairprice-group-scales-ai-powered-retail-to-48-singapore-supermarkets/) — pulled 14 July 2026.
