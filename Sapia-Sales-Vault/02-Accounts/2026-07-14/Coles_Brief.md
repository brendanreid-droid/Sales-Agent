### 4. Technology Stack Sourcing
- ATS/HRIS: **SAP SuccessFactors HXM Suite** is Coles' core people system of record, implemented as part of a broader SAP S/4HANA and SAP Ariba digital transformation. Layered on top is **Phenom**, an SAP-partner AI-powered "Intelligent Talent Experience" platform, providing two-way integration back into SuccessFactors so SuccessFactors remains the single source of truth. This combination is confirmed as still active as of October 2025 (Coles' Head of Talent Acquisition was a named speaker at Phenom's Customer Obsession Day) and referenced again in his own recent LinkedIn activity ("optimising the value from our Phenom solution"). Sources: [Coles Reimagines Hiring with AI & Automation — SAP News Center](https://news.sap.com/2023/12/coles-reimagines-volume-hiring-with-ai-and-automation/), pulled 14 July 2026; [Human Resource Success Stories Take Center Stage at Phenom's Customer Obsession Day — Phenom press release](https://www.phenom.com/press-release/human-resource-success-stories-take-center-stage-at-phenoms-customer), pulled 14 July 2026; Mike Virgo, Head of Talent Acquisition, Coles Group — [LinkedIn](https://au.linkedin.com/in/michael-j-virgo), pulled 14 July 2026.
- What Phenom actually does at Coles: an AI-powered chatbot on the careers site to guide candidates, and **Phenom Fit Score**, which ranks and matches candidates to roles based on location/travel distance, availability and working rights, surfacing the closest matches to recruiters. Two purpose-built RPA bots, "Ashbotty" (working rights verification) and "Usain Bot" (onboarding and pending-recruit management), automate downstream admin. Source: [SAP News Center](https://news.sap.com/2023/12/coles-reimagines-volume-hiring-with-ai-and-automation/), pulled 14 July 2026.
- Critically: **no evidence found of a structured, competency-based behavioural screening or interview layer.** Phenom Fit Score is a matching/ranking algorithm built on logistics criteria (location, availability, working rights), not a candidate assessment of behavioural fit, soft skills or likely retention. This is a genuine gap even inside an otherwise sophisticated, multi-tool TA stack. UNKNOWN whether any separate psychometric or video interview tool sits downstream; none could be verified.
- Recruitment tools/assessment: AI chatbot (careers site), Phenom Fit Score (matching), Ashbotty and Usain Bot (RPA automation). No video interview or structured assessment tool found in public sourcing.
- Sourcing channels: Coles' own careers sites (careers.colesgroup.com.au, colescareers.com.au, thecolesgroup.com/careers), Seek, Indeed, Glassdoor, ZipRecruiter, Jooble, plus an Expression of Interest model for store roles. Coles' career site has won "Australia's Best Careers Site" twice. Source: [SAP News Center](https://news.sap.com/2023/12/coles-reimagines-volume-hiring-with-ai-and-automation/), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** Coles' TA function already runs a genuinely sophisticated stack (SAP SuccessFactors plus Phenom Fit Score plus two RPA bots) processing close to 1 million applications a year, but that stack is built entirely for matching and routing (who is nearby, available and eligible to work), not for assessing behavioural fit or likely retention. Recruiters get a ranked shortlist faster, but still have no structured, consistent way to know which of those ranked candidates will actually be a good, stable hire. (Source for application volume and Fit Score mechanics: [SAP News Center](https://news.sap.com/2023/12/coles-reimagines-volume-hiring-with-ai-and-automation/), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Business Pain:** Coles is in a period of volume-led growth outpacing the market (supermarkets +4%, eCommerce +24.8% in Q3 FY26) while simultaneously managing active cost pressure from fuel and supplier prices and continuing heavy capital investment in automation (two A$1 billion automated distribution centres already live, more automation flagged). Every store and fulfilment role filled with a matched-but-unassessed candidate carries direct downstream cost risk (turnover, retraining, supervisor time) at precisely the moment growth is straining frontline and picking capacity. (Sources: [Motley Fool AU Q3 FY26 results](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/05/01/coles-group-shares-in-focus-after-q3-fy26-sales-rise-3-1/); [Future Food Systems — Coles and Woolworths move to robot warehouses](https://www.futurefoodsystems.com.au/coles-and-woolworths-are-moving-to-robot-warehouses-and-on-demand-labour-as-home-deliveries-soar/), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Personal Pain:** Mike Virgo's own recent public commentary shows he is actively thinking about where AI changes recruiter work ("sourcing, screening and scheduling will be heavily automated") and is personally focused on "optimising the value" already invested in Phenom, which suggests a mandate to prove further ROI from the existing AI investment rather than bolt on a competing platform. Deb Yates, one year into the CPO seat, is likely still shaping her own people-strategy agenda and looking for a concrete, provable initiative to point to. (Sources: Mike Virgo LinkedIn activity, pulled 14 July 2026; Deb Yates appointment coverage, [HR Today](https://hrtoday.in/deborah-yates-appointed-as-chief-people-officer-at-coles-group/), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Career Pain:** Coles publicly reports and tracks diversity and gender-balance metrics (FY25 mean gender pay gap 5.9%, 42.7% women in leadership, goal of 40/40/20 gender balance in leadership) under Equileap and Australian Workplace Equality Awards recognition. A CPO and Head of TA operating a large-scale, resume/matching-driven hiring engine with no independently auditable, bias-tested behavioural assessment layer carry residual fairness and compliance exposure on exactly the metrics they are publicly scored against. (Source: [Gender Equality at Coles — Prosple Australia](https://au.prosple.com/graduate-employers/coles/diversity/gender-equality), pulled 14 July 2026; [Coles Diversity and Inclusion Policy — colesgroup.com.au](https://www.colesgroup.com.au/FormBuilder/_Resource/_module/ir5sKeTxxEOndzdh00hWJw/file/Diversity_Policy.pdf), pulled 14 July 2026.) UNKNOWN whether Coles leadership has named this fairness/compliance gap explicitly; this is Sapia's inference from the confirmed tooling profile, not a company statement.

- **Failed/Current Alternatives likely in use:** No evidence of a dedicated pre-hire behavioural assessment, video interview, or psychometric tool currently deployed. The current "alternative" to structured assessment is effectively resume/profile matching via Phenom Fit Score plus recruiter judgement at shortlist stage, which is a logistics-matching approach rather than a fit/retention-prediction approach. UNKNOWN whether Coles has trialled HireVue, Sapia, or any competitor screening tool historically; no public evidence found. Given the depth of existing investment in Phenom and SAP, any Sapia conversation needs to be positioned as complementary to the existing stack (sitting downstream of Fit Score matching, feeding structured behavioural data back into SuccessFactors) rather than a rip-and-replace.

### 9. The ONE Sourcing / Buying Signal Worth Opening On
- Coles has just made itself the most publicly AI-forward major retailer in Australia (OpenAI/ChatGPT Enterprise, first at scale, board-visible, rolling out February 2026) while its Head of Talent Acquisition is simultaneously and openly focused on "optimising the value" of its existing Phenom investment, an AI matching tool that ranks candidates on logistics fit (location, availability, working rights) but does not assess behavioural fit or likely retention. That is the clearest opening: an organisation primed and mandated to extend its AI investment in hiring, running near 1 million applications a year through a stack with a genuine, provable gap between "matched" and "assessed." Sources: as cited in Sections 3, 4 and 8 above.

### 10. Suggested Opener Angle (1 Sentence)
- Given Coles' recent first-mover AI partnership with OpenAI and Mike Virgo's own public focus on extracting more value from the Phenom investment already in place, Coles is well positioned to close the gap between its AI-powered candidate matching (Phenom Fit Score, ranking on location and availability) and a structured, bias-tested assessment of who is actually the best behavioural fit and most likely to stay, the layer that stack does not currently cover.
