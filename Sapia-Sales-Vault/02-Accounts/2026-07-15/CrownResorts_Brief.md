# Company Brief: Crown Resorts
**Date:** 2026-07-15
**Territory:** APAC (ANZ)

### 1. Company Snapshot
- Stage: Privately held, owned by Blackstone (acquired February 2022)
- Headcount: Over 20,000 people across its resorts in more than 700 different roles, per Crown's own careers page ([crownresorts.com.au/careers](https://www.crownresorts.com.au/careers), pulled 2026-07-15). Note: dispatch brief for this session cited approximately 18,000, in the same order of magnitude as Crown's own public figure; treat the higher, company-stated figure as authoritative.
- Revenue Band: UNKNOWN (Crown has been private since the 2022 Blackstone acquisition and no longer files ASX-standard public financials; a specific current revenue figure could not be verified)
- HQ Location: Melbourne, Victoria, Australia
- Key Markets: Australia only, three integrated resorts (Crown Melbourne, Crown Perth, Crown Sydney)

### 2. Funding History
- Total Raised: Not applicable, acquired outright by Blackstone for approximately AU$8.9 billion, completed 13 February 2022 ([Blackstone press release](https://www.blackstone.com/news/press/blackstone-completes-acquisition-of-crown-resorts-in-the-firms-largest-investment-to-date-in-asia/), pulled 2026-07-15)
- Last Raise Date & Round: Not applicable (private equity ownership, not a raise)
- Lead Investors: Blackstone Inc.

### 3. Recent News & Trigger Events (Last 90 Days, with earlier context flagged where still materially relevant)
- Crown Perth is recruiting for its new Urban Food District, described as its biggest venue investment in nearly a decade, with roughly 100 local frontline roles (bar attendants, cook alones across pizza, burgers, sushi and Vietnamese cuisine, junior sous chefs, venue supervisors) and a scheduled mass intake date of 19 January 2026 ([GGB Magazine](https://ggbmagazine.com/articles/australias-crown-perth-adds-new-food-venues/), pulled 2026-07-15).
- Crown's group-wide Transformation Plan (covering harm minimisation, financial crime prevention, governance and organisational culture reform) runs through 31 December 2026, with the Victorian Gambling and Casino Control Commission's next Periodic Investigation into Crown's suitability scheduled for 2027, meaning Crown is currently under active regulator scrutiny of its culture and people practices ([Vixio / VGCCC coverage](https://www.vixio.com/insights/gc-regulator-rules-crown-resorts-can-keep-melbourne-casino), pulled 2026-07-15).
- Context (outside the 90-day window but still operative): Louise Tebbutt was appointed Chief People and Culture Officer in September 2024 and is leading Crown's HR and culture transformation across all three resorts ([HR Today](https://hrtoday.in/louise-tebbutt-appointed-as-chief-people-and-culture-officer-at-crown-resorts/), pulled 2026-07-15).
