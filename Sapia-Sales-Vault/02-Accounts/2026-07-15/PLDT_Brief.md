### 1. Company Snapshot
- Stage: Public company, dual-listed (PSE: TEL, and ADRs on NYSE: PHI). Source: [PLDT Inc. Form 20-F FY2025, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000078150/000119312526182147/phi-20251231.htm), pulled 15 July 2026.
- Headcount: **14,304 employees as of 31 December 2025** (10,456 fixed line, 3,848 wireless), per the FY2025 Form 20-F. This is up from 14,904 reported for FY2024 in the prior filing, a modest year-on-year decline, not growth. Sources: [PLDT Form 20-F FY2025, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000078150/000119312526182147/phi-20251231.htm); [PLDT Form 20-F FY2024, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/78150/000095017025038448/phi-20241231.htm) — both pulled 15 July 2026.
- Revenue: FY2025 gross service revenues **Php212.2 billion** (+2% year on year); net service revenues Php196.2 billion (+1%); Data and Broadband made up 85% of service revenues (Php166.5 billion); consolidated EBITDA hit a record **Php111.2 billion** (52% margin, +3%). Maya (PLDT's digital finance arm) posted its first full year of profitability, contributing Php1.7 billion net income. Source: [Minichart — PLDT Reports Record 2025 Financial Results](https://www.minichart.com.sg/2026/02/26/pldt-reports-record-2025-financial-results-revenue-fiber-maya-profitability-and-sustainability-highlights/), pulled 15 July 2026.
- Q1 2026 momentum: revenues up 2% to Php56.5 billion; EBITDA up 2% to Php28.3 billion; net income down 2% to Php8.9 billion. Source: [PLDT Form 6-K, Q1 2026, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000078150/000119312526222745/phi_6-k_1q2026.htm), pulled 15 July 2026.
- HQ Location: Makati City, Metro Manila, Philippines (registered address per SEC filing). Source: [PLDT Form 20-F FY2025, SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0000078150/000119312526182147/phi-20251231.htm), pulled 15 July 2026.
- Key Markets: Domestic Philippines only, across fixed line/fibre broadband (PLDT Home), wireless/mobile (Smart Communications), enterprise/ICT (PLDT Enterprise, ePLDT), and digital finance (Maya). Source: same 20-F filing, pulled 15 July 2026.

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** PLDT is publicly investing in AI across customer support (Amdocs-built OSS) and workforce learning (Smart's AI Pioneer: Learning Champion finalist nod), but no public evidence shows that AI investment has reached the recruitment screening step itself. With no confirmed assessment/screening vendor in place, the likely default is still manual CV review and recruiter-led phone screening for a workforce of 14,304 (down slightly from 14,904 in FY2024, per Section 1), which sits awkwardly against a freshly-won Best Employer Brand title.
- **Business Pain:** A record-EBITDA, growth-mode business (Maya's first profitable year, continued fibre/data growth) needs technical and digital talent to keep scaling, and PLDT's own headcount modestly contracted year on year even as the business grew financially, a sign that hiring capacity, not budget, may be the constraint. UNKNOWN whether this is explicitly attributed to TA bottleneck; this is an inference from the headcount-versus-revenue divergence, not a company statement.
- **Personal Pain:** CPO Gina Ordoñez has just publicly staked her team's reputation on being "connected, aligned, recognized, and empowered" (the WE:CARE framework) days before this brief was written. Any visible gap between that employer-brand story and the actual candidate-facing hiring experience is now a live reputational exposure for her team, right after a public win.
- **Career Pain:** Having delivered a headline industry recognition, Ordoñez faces pressure to keep the "people" agenda visibly modern and AI-forward as the rest of the business (customer ops, learning) publicly leans into AI, so recruitment risks becoming the one part of the employee/candidate journey that looks manual by comparison. UNKNOWN whether this has been raised internally; this is Sapia's inference from the public pattern, not a confirmed statement.

- **Failed/Current Alternatives:** No confirmed AI screening or assessment tool in public evidence, unlike Globe's confirmed HireVue deployment. Most likely current state is manual CV screening and recruiter-led phone screens (Sapia's most common displacement category per `00-ICP/ICP_Master_Profile.md`). This makes PLDT a plausible green-field opportunity rather than a hard displacement, though this is an inference from absence of evidence, not a confirmed fact.
