# Company Brief: Hungry Jack's® Pty Ltd
**Date:** 14 July 2026
**Territory:** APAC (Australia, single-market focus; parent group Competitive Foods Australia/CFAL Group also holds NZ operations)

> **Entity note:** The target is **Hungry Jack's Pty Ltd**, the Australian master franchisee of Burger King, wholly owned by **Competitive Foods Australia** (trading as CFAL Group), which is privately owned and chaired by Jack Cowin. Jack Cowin is also the largest shareholder (approximately 25 percent) of ASX-listed Domino's Pizza Enterprises (already researched separately in `Sapia-Sales-Vault/02-Accounts/Dominos_Brief.md`) and stepped in as interim Executive Chair of Domino's after CEO Mark van Dyck's abrupt resignation in December 2025. This is a useful relationship-mapping note (same ultimate controlling individual across two Tier 1 Sapia target accounts) but Hungry Jack's and Domino's Pizza Enterprises are run as fully separate operating businesses with separate People/HR leadership; do not conflate them in outreach. Source: [Jack Cowin takes control of Domino's — The Nightly](https://thenightly.com.au/business/hungry-jacks-founder-jack-cowin-takes-control-of-dominos-as-new-ceo-mark-van-dyck-abruptly-exits-c-19221272), pulled 14 July 2026; [Jack Cowin — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jack_Cowin), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 1. Company Snapshot
- Stage: Private company. Wholly owned subsidiary of Competitive Foods Australia (CFAL Group), which itself is privately held by Jack Cowin under licence from Restaurant Brands International (global Burger King franchisor) to operate the Burger King brand in Australia as "Hungry Jack's". Source: [Hungry Jack's — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungry_Jack's), pulled 14 July 2026.
- Headcount: **Approximately 30,000 employees across Australia**, per the most recent (2025) Workday customer story and a corroborating 06 August 2025 HR trade press feature. Note this is up from "more than 22,000 employees" cited in a March 2024 Workday press release and roughly 16,500 in 2019 per Wikipedia, and from the 25,000 to 26,000 figures cited by Competitive Foods/CFAL Group business coverage. Treat 30,000 as the most current, source-verified figure; the internal vault's Tier 1 list estimate of 19,000 (`00-ICP/Target_Account_Analysis.md`) is now understated and should be corrected on next vault refresh. Sources: [Workday customer story — Hungry Jack's Flips the Script on Hiring and Workforce Planning](https://www.workday.com/en-au/customer-stories/a-h/hungry-jacks-flips-script-on-hiring-workforce-planning.html), pulled 14 July 2026; [HRD Australia — From spreadsheets to hiring in 24 hours](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/hr-technology/from-spreadsheets-to-hiring-in-24-hours-inside-hungry-jacks-workforce-transformation/542675), pulled 14 July 2026 (published 06 Aug 2025); [Workday newsroom press release, 4 March 2024](https://en-au.newsroom.workday.com/press-releases?item=122804), pulled 14 July 2026.
- Revenue Band: Parent Competitive Foods Australia reported approximately A$2.4 billion group revenue (18 percent year-on-year jump) with net profit of A$74.1 million, per most recent available figures. Hungry Jack's Pty Ltd entity-level revenue cited separately at approximately A$1.4 billion (2026 company filings summary). Sources: [Grokipedia — Competitive Foods Australia](https://grokipedia.com/page/Competitive_Foods_Australia), pulled 14 July 2026; [RocketReach — Hungry Jack's Pty Ltd](https://rocketreach.co/hungry-jacksr-pty-ltd-profile_b5c03402f42e082a), pulled 14 July 2026.
- Store network: **More than 450 restaurants** nationwide (some sources cite 480+), serving over 2.2 million Australians weekly. Approximately 75 to 85 percent of stores are **company-operated**, not independently franchised, a materially different structure to Domino's Pizza Enterprises' predominantly franchised model. Stated growth plan is 20+ new store openings per year, with a long-term upper ambition of 700 stores. Sources: [HRD Australia](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/hr-technology/from-spreadsheets-to-hiring-in-24-hours-inside-hungry-jacks-workforce-transformation/542675), pulled 14 July 2026; [Hungry Jack's — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungry_Jack's), pulled 14 July 2026; [Forbes Australia — Jack Cowin](https://www.forbes.com.au/news/billionaires/hungry-jacks-billionaire-jack-cowin-on-his-success-strategy/), pulled 14 July 2026.
- HQ Location: Sydney, New South Wales, Australia (CPO and CEO both listed as Sydney-based). Source: [LinkedIn — Chris Green](https://au.linkedin.com/in/chris-green-5255b313), pulled 14 July 2026.
- Key Markets: Australia only for the Hungry Jack's brand (single-country operation); parent CFAL Group has separate interests. This is Sapia's core ANZ market. Source: [Hungry Jack's — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungry_Jack's), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** Even after a substantial, multi-year Workday investment that solved *intake speed and volume* (300 percent more applications, 24-hour time-to-hire), Hungry Jack's own CPO has publicly confirmed that, until recently, restaurant-level managers, not professional recruiters, were personally "swamped" doing CV screening, interview scheduling and onboarding admin by hand. Workday's Talent Acquisition module speeds up the *pipeline*, but there is no public evidence it adds a structured, standardised behavioural or competency assessment layer at the point of screening; the underlying, on-the-ground screening decision at 450+ stores likely still rests on inconsistent manager judgement. (Source: [HRD Australia](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/hr-technology/from-spreadsheets-to-hiring-in-24-hours-inside-hungry-jacks-workforce-transformation/542675), pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Business Pain:** Hungry Jack's now receives 10,000+ applications a week against a hiring need of roughly 300 people a week, a funnel ratio of over 30:1. At that ratio, without a structured, scalable, bias-controlled screening layer, either (a) restaurant managers are drowning in volume despite the faster Workday pipeline, or (b) shortlisting quality is being sacrificed for speed, both of which directly threaten the company's stated growth plan of 20+ new store openings a year across a workforce that is approximately 75 to 85 percent company-operated (meaning Hungry Jack's, not third-party franchisees, directly wears the cost of every bad hire, every store's turnover, and every unfilled shift). (Sources: [Workday customer story](https://www.workday.com/en-au/customer-stories/a-h/hungry-jacks-flips-script-on-hiring-workforce-planning.html); [HRD Australia](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/hr-technology/from-spreadsheets-to-hiring-in-24-hours-inside-hungry-jacks-workforce-transformation/542675) — both pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Personal Pain:** Jenny McKie has staked her public reputation and a named award ambition (ABA Employer of Choice) on this transformation being seen as a full success, not just a faster application form. Having already delivered the "speed" chapter of the story (Phase 1 to 2 of Workday, live since July 2025), she is now on the hook to show the "quality and retention" chapter, especially with roughly 80 percent of the workforce reportedly under age 20 (a demographic where mobile-first, low-friction, non-intimidating candidate experience is critical to both completion rates and employer brand). A visible AI-in-hiring misstep is also a live reputational risk for her given the public backlash Hungry Jack's has already absorbed over the "Jackie" AI drive-thru trial and job-loss criticism; any pitch must be framed as augmenting her managers' judgement and protecting young workers' access to first jobs, not replacing headcount. (Sources: [HRD Australia](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/hr-technology/from-spreadsheets-to-hiring-in-24-hours-inside-hungry-jacks-workforce-transformation/542675); [Yahoo Finance AU](https://au.finance.yahoo.com/news/millions-of-aussies-warned-as-hungry-jacks-replaces-drive-thru-workers-with-ai-disappear-035538602.html) — both pulled 14 July 2026.)
- **Root Cause:** Hungry Jack's has invested heavily and successfully in the *administrative* layer of hiring (Workday: forms, workflow, pipeline speed, internal mobility, workforce planning) but has publicly disclosed no equivalent investment in the *decision-quality* layer (structured, bias-controlled, competency-based candidate screening at the point of application). This is the classic gap Sapia is built to close, and because Workday is already the live ATS, a Sapia deployment is a natively-integrated add-on to an existing, well-funded transformation programme rather than a rip-and-replace pitch. UNKNOWN whether Hungry Jack's leadership has named this specific gap internally; this is Sapia's inference from the confirmed public record, not a direct company statement.

### 8. Leadership Stance & Themes
- CPO Jenny McKie's public commentary consistently centres on **people-first culture, internal career pathways for young workers, and "forever forward" embrace of technology and AI** to remove admin burden from managers so they can focus on people. Explicit quote: "What we are most excited about is the 17-year-old crew member who has a real path to a restaurant manager role," and "We're excited about what AI will enable, and the business is embracing the change." Sources: [HRD Australia](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/hr-technology/from-spreadsheets-to-hiring-in-24-hours-inside-hungry-jacks-workforce-transformation/542675); [Workday customer story](https://www.workday.com/en-au/customer-stories/a-h/hungry-jacks-flips-script-on-hiring-workforce-planning.html) — both pulled 14 July 2026.
- Recurring theme: **data-driven workforce planning and skills-gap visibility** ("With Workday's analytics and reporting, we can identify skill gaps, anticipate future workforce needs, and implement targeted training programs" — McKie). This is a CHRO-persona hook per `00-ICP/Persona_Maps.md`: board-level, ROI-and-data-led framing will land better with her than a pure speed pitch, since she has already won the speed argument publicly.
- Company-wide theme: **willingness to trial customer-facing AI, but exposure to public backlash when AI appears to threaten entry-level youth jobs** (the "Jackie" drive-thru controversy). This is a genuine sensitivity Sapia's messaging should proactively address: Sapia augments manager decision-making and is explicitly "Ethical by Design" (text-only, no video/audio, fairness as a core success metric per `01-Product/Product_Overview.md`), a materially different and lower-risk category of AI than a voice-ordering job-replacement tool, and this contrast is worth making explicitly if the topic comes up in conversation (not necessarily in a cold email).
- No public leadership commentary found specifically naming candidate screening quality, shortlist consistency, or structured assessment as a current problem; this is Sapia's own inference (Section 6), not a stated company position.

### 9. The ONE Sourcing / Buying Signal Worth Opening On
- Hungry Jack's has just finished proving, in its own public Workday case study, that it can move hiring *speed* from weeks to 24 hours and *volume* up 300 percent, with CPO Jenny McKie explicitly stating the next chapter is about AI-enabled recruitment quality and an Employer of Choice ambition. That is a company that has already done the hard internal work of justifying HR tech investment, has a named, publicly accountable executive sponsor, runs on a Workday ATS that is a confirmed native Sapia integration, and has a public record of managers previously drowning in manual screening. This is as close to a warm, self-identified "what's next" signal as an outbound target gets. Sources: as cited throughout Sections 3, 4, 6 and 8.
