# Company Brief: Ramsay Health Care Limited (ASX: RHC)
**Date:** 14 July 2026
**Territory:** APAC (Australia primary market; group also operates in France via Ramsay Santé, the UK, and Malaysia/Indonesia via the Ramsay Sime Darby joint venture)

> **Entity note:** The target is **Ramsay Health Care Limited** (ASX:RHC), HQ Level 7, Tower B, 7 Westbourne Street, St Leonards, New South Wales. Ramsay is in the process of demerging its 52.8% stake in European subsidiary Ramsay Santé (targeted completion Q4 calendar 2026), which will sharpen the group's public reporting and management focus onto the standalone Australian private hospital business. This brief focuses on the Australian operation as Sapia's clearest entry point, consistent with Sapia's ANZ vendor base. Source: [Ramsay Health Care unveils plan to separate Ramsay Santé — The Motley Fool AU](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/02/20/ramsay-health-care-unveils-plan-to-separate-ramsay-sante-in-strategic-shift/), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 3. Recent News & Trigger Events (widened to ~6 months given density of activity)
- **Ramsay Santé demerger progressing:** Announced circa 20 February 2026, Ramsay is separating its 52.8% stake in European subsidiary Ramsay Santé via an in-specie distribution to shareholders. Timeline: demerger documents due October 2026, shareholder vote November 2026, completion targeted Q4 calendar 2026. Strategic rationale is explicitly to let management focus on the core Australian hospital business. Sources: [The Motley Fool AU](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/02/20/ramsay-health-care-unveils-plan-to-separate-ramsay-sante-in-strategic-shift/); [Stockwirex — In-specie distribution](https://stockwirex.com/asx-stock-news/biotech-health/rhc-ramsay-sante-separation-in-specie-distribution-2026/); [TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/ramsay-health-care-to-spin-off-european-unit-ramsay-sante-to-shareholders) — all pulled 14 July 2026.
- **National Capital Private Hospital acquisition (from Healthscope receivership):** Healthscope, Australia's second-largest private hospital operator, entered receivership in May 2025 owing more than A$1.6 billion. Ramsay is acquiring Healthscope's National Capital Private Hospital in Canberra for A$251 million, its first hospital investment in the ACT. ACCC approved the deal on 13 February 2026; completion is anticipated in Q1 of FY27 (i.e., from around July 2026, the current period). This means an entire hospital's clinical and support workforce is transitioning into Ramsay employment right now. Sources: [Region Canberra — ACCC approval](https://region.com.au/national-capital-private-hospitals-future-secure-as-accc-approves-sale/942402/); [Canberra Times — $251m acquisition](https://www.canberratimes.com.au/story/9140631/ramsay-health-care-acquires-act-hospital-for-251m/); [Sharecafe](https://www.sharecafe.com.au/2026/02/13/ramsay-acquires-national-capital-private-hospital-2/) — all pulled 14 July 2026.
- **Two new/expanded Australian sites opened, February 2026:** Northern Private Hospital, Epping VIC (A$145 million, 70 beds, cardiac catheterisation lab, opened 5 February 2026) and the A$166 million expansion of Ramsay Private at Joondalup Health Campus, WA (opened February 2026, now one of Australia's largest health campuses). Both require substantial new nursing and allied health recruitment. Sources: [Hospital Management — Northern Private Hospital](https://www.hospitalmanagement.net/newsletters/ramsay-northern-private-hospital/?type=Spotlight); [Ramsay News — Joondalup expansion](https://www.ramsaynews.com.au/General-News/World-class-private-health-care-facilities-open-at-Ramsay-Private-at-Joondalup-Health-Campus) — pulled 14 July 2026.
- **NSW Nurses and Midwives Enterprise Agreement reaches nominal expiry 30 June 2026:** The prior EA was only settled in 2025 after more than 250 days of industrial action and three no-votes, ultimately delivering a 16% pay rise over three years plus backpay from July 2023. Separately, the Fair Work Commission found Ramsay had been miscalculating nurses' and midwives' annual leave payments for up to six years, with an estimated total backpay liability "well over $10 million". Bargaining for the next NSW agreement is due to reopen from the current expiry date, landing directly in this research window. Sources: [Lamp Online — Ramsay nurses and midwives strike deal](https://thelamp.com.au/specialities/privatesector/ramsay-nurses-and-midwives-strike-deal-after-two-year-fight/); [Lamp Online — backpay win](https://thelamp.com.au/specialities/privatesector/union-win-ramsay-nurses-and-midwives-to-receive-annual-leave-backpay-after-fwc-win/); [NSWNMA — Ramsay NSW Nurses and Midwives EA 2023-2026 PDF](https://www.nswnma.asn.au/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Ramsay-Health-Care-New-South-Wales-Nurses-and-Midwives-Enterprise-Agreement-2023-2026.pdf) — all pulled 14 July 2026.
- **New Group CEO context (background, not a fresh-90-day trigger but directly relevant):** Natalie Davis (previously a senior Woolworths Group executive, including Managing Director Woolworths Supermarkets) was appointed Group CEO and Managing Director, commencing as CEO-elect 1 October 2024 and taking the role fully from 2 December 2024. She has since led a refresh of the "Ramsay 2030" strategy focused on "innovation, sustainable growth, capital discipline and a culture of performance". Source: [Ramsay Health Care — Natalie Davis bio](https://www.ramsayhealth.com/en/about/natalie-davis/), pulled 14 July 2026.
