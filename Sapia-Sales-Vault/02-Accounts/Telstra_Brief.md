### 3. Recent News & Trigger Events (Last ~6 Months)
- **Active, multi-wave restructuring through FY26:** Telstra cut 2,356 roles across calendar 2025, with a further approximately 550 to 800 roles targeted for FY26 (roughly 2% of the workforce), primarily to simplify and restructure the Telstra Enterprise division. Around 440 roles are in scope from a proposal to shift work to Indian IT services firm Infosys, and approximately 200 more roles are in scope under Telstra's AI/data joint venture with Accenture, with some roles offshored to India. Announced circa 11 February 2026 alongside H1 FY26 results. Sources: [Telstra flags hundreds of job cuts in fresh restructure — HRD/HCAmag](https://www.hcamag.com/au/specialisation/industrial-relations/telstra-flags-hundreds-of-job-cuts-in-fresh-restructure/565094), pulled 14 July 2026; [Telstra to axe 800 more jobs, move some roles to India — ACS Information Age](https://ia.acs.org.au/article/2026/telstra-to-axe-800-more-jobs--move-some-roles-to-india.html), pulled 14 July 2026; [ACS Information Age — H1 FY26 results](https://ia.acs.org.au/article/2026/telstra-posts--1-2b-half-year-profit-as-1-000-jobs-cut.html), pulled 14 July 2026.
- **CEO Vicki Brady on record that Telstra will be a structurally smaller workforce by 2030**, citing AI-driven efficiency ("a significant unlock") as a factor, while stressing no single role has been directly and solely replaced by AI to date. Positioned as "a whole of business transformation" spanning customer engagement, team empowerment and network operations. Source: [Telstra CEO Vicki Brady gives insight into the telco's AI journey — CRN](https://www.crn.com.au/news/2026/ai/telstra-ceo-vicky-brady-ai-microsoft-world-tour), pulled 14 July 2026.
- **Labour cost discipline is explicit and quantified:** H1 FY26 labour costs fell 9% to A$181 million, partly attributed to lower headcount, while redundancy expenses rose to A$63 million in the same half. This signals the People function is under direct pressure to run a leaner cost base while still executing high-volume hiring for retained and growth functions (mobile, digital infrastructure, AI/data). Source: [ARN — Telstra posts strong first half FY26 profit amid job cuts](https://www.arnnet.com.au/article/4134212/telstra-posts-strong-first-half-fy26-profit-amid-job-cuts.html), pulled 14 July 2026.
- **Mobile remains Telstra's clear growth engine** (H1 FY26 mobile revenue up 3.6% to A$5.7 billion), meaning frontline retail and customer service consultant hiring for stores and contact centres remains commercially important even as back-office/enterprise roles are cut — i.e. this is not an across-the-board hiring freeze, but a reallocation of hiring effort toward customer-facing and technical roles. Source: [ARN — H1 FY26 results](https://www.arnnet.com.au/article/4134212/telstra-posts-strong-first-half-fy26-profit-amid-job-cuts.html), pulled 14 July 2026.
- **T25 strategy (2021 to 2025) explicitly named "talent acquisition, mobility and career management" as a strategic focus area**, reporting 1,500+ new hires against target in software engineering, data analytics, cyber security and AI during the T25 period. The successor "Connected Future 30" (T30) strategy continues to emphasise digitisation of the workforce and IT platform modernisation. Source: [Telstra — Our corporate strategy](https://www.telstra.com.au/aboutus/our-company/our-corporate-strategy), pulled 14 July 2026; [Strategic Foresight — Telstra's T30 Strategy](https://blog.sharmavishal.com/2025/06/telstras-t30-strategy-balanced-but.html), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** Telstra's frontline hiring (retail Customer Service and Sales Consultants, contact centre roles) runs through a Workday-hosted application with a HireVue video/digital interview as the first screening gate. Candidate-side evidence shows real friction at this exact step: only 50% positive interview experience ratings for the Sales and Customer Service Consultant pathway, and candidate commentary describing the process as impersonal, long, and lacking any performance feedback loop. This is a direct, named displacement opportunity, not an inferred one. Sources: Section 4 citations above.
- **Business Pain:** Telstra is simultaneously cutting roughly 550 to 800 roles in FY26 in Enterprise and back-office functions while keeping mobile and customer-facing hiring active, because mobile revenue (its main growth engine, up 3.6% in H1 FY26) depends on well-staffed, well-performing retail stores and contact centres. Every point of friction or drop-off in the HireVue-gated frontline hiring funnel directly threatens the customer-facing capacity Telstra is relying on to protect its one clearly growing revenue line, at precisely the moment overall headcount and labour cost are under intense board and investor scrutiny (labour costs down 9% to A$181m in H1 FY26, redundancy costs up to A$63m). A slow or leaky frontline screening funnel is a direct tax on the part of the business Telstra needs to keep strong while it restructures everywhere else. Sources: Section 3 citations above.
- **Personal Pain:** Kathryn van der Merwe is managing this tension directly — leading the people function through Telstra's most intense cost-out and restructuring period in years (2,356 roles cut in 2025 alone, hundreds more flagged for FY26, some work moving offshore to India), while CEO Vicki Brady has publicly flagged a structurally smaller workforce by 2030 partly enabled by AI. Van der Merwe is very likely balancing constant redundancy and redeployment programme execution with the need to still recruit well for the retained, customer-facing and technical workforce that is meant to carry Telstra's growth strategy (T30 / Connected Future 30) — a genuinely difficult "shrink here, grow there, do both flawlessly" mandate. Sources: [CRN — Vicki Brady AI journey](https://www.crn.com.au/news/2026/ai/telstra-ceo-vicky-brady-ai-microsoft-world-tour), pulled 14 July 2026; Section 3 citations above.
- **Root Cause:** Telstra has invested heavily and publicly in digitising HR and recruitment workflows for years (legacy 2019 systems consolidation, explicit T25 strategic focus on talent acquisition and mobility), but the actual candidate-facing screening tool at the frontline layer (HireVue) is producing measurably below-average candidate experience outcomes (50% positive) at the exact volume tier (retail and customer service consultants across ~265 stores plus contact centre roles) where Sapia's text-based, no-video, structured competency approach is purpose-built. UNKNOWN whether Telstra leadership has explicitly named the HireVue candidate experience gap internally; this is Sapia's evidence-based inference from public candidate review data, not a direct company statement.
