# Company Brief: Australia Post (Australian Postal Corporation)
**Date:** 14 July 2026
**Territory:** APAC (Australia, domestic universal service obligation plus international parcels/express)

> **Entity note:** Australia Post is a wholly Commonwealth Government owned corporation established under the Australian Postal Corporation Act 1989, prescribed as a Government Business Enterprise (GBE) under the Public Governance, Performance and Accountability Act 2013. It is not ASX listed and has no external shareholders. It is accountable to two Shareholder Ministers, the Minister for Finance and the Minister for Communications, and is subject to Commonwealth Procurement Rules and periodic Senate Estimates scrutiny. This is a new lookalike account, not currently a Sapia customer. Source: [Australia Post — Department of Finance](https://www.finance.gov.au/government/government-business-enterprises/australia-post), pulled 14 July 2026; [Australian Postal Corporation Act 1989](https://www.legislation.gov.au/Details/C2023C00388), pulled 14 July 2026.

### 3. Recent News & Trigger Events (Last ~6 Months, widened given density of signal)
- **"Post30" four year restructuring strategy announced by CEO Paul Graham**, delivered via internal video address to the workforce (60,000+ staff) in mid July 2026. Graham called it "probably the most important strategy in the 215 years of Australia Post," described the period ahead as a "battle for our lives," and said the business must "eliminate, simplify, or automate" processes using AI, or "die on the vine." Post30 escalates the prior Post26 program and pivots the business from a traditional postal service toward same day and on demand parcel delivery to compete directly with Amazon; Graham said the "last letter" may be delivered "potentially within the next decade." Source: [Australia Post CEO escalates "Post 30" restructuring drive — World Socialist Web Site, 14 July 2026](https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2026/07/14/flwf-j14.html), pulled 14 July 2026. - **Acquisition of Rendr**, an AI powered last mile "delivery orchestration" platform that coordinates deliveries across multiple courier services, referenced as part of the Post30 automation push. Source: same WSWS article, pulled 14 July 2026.

Roberts spent close to three decades in senior finance and operations leadership at DHL, most recently as CEO of ASMO (a DHL and Aramco joint venture) in Saudi Arabia. ### 6. 4-Layer Pain Points Mapping
- **Surface Pain:** Australia Post runs a dual speed hiring model on a confirmed Avature ATS: a lean permanent Talent Acquisition team supplemented by a large temporary "campaign consultant" surge workforce to manually process thousands of seasonal casual applications (historically up to 5,000 hires across a 12 week Christmas window, per a former TA leader's account), with no confirmed structured pre hire screening or chat based assessment layer sitting in front of that volume. Source: Sections 4 and 5.
- **Business Pain:** CEO Paul Graham has just told the entire workforce that Post30 is a "battle for our lives," explicitly repositioning the business toward same day and on demand parcel delivery to compete with Amazon, while letter volumes structurally decline and fuel costs have risen sharply this year (from $35.7 million in February to $56.5 million in April 2026 alone). Every network, warehouse or delivery vacancy left unfilled during this pivot works directly against the same day delivery promise Post30 is being staked on, at the exact moment the business has publicly framed its survival as contingent on speed and cost discipline. Source: WSWS Post30 article; Senate Estimates statement, both pulled 14 July 2026.
- **Personal Pain:** Susan Davies' People & Culture function is very likely absorbing enormous seasonal workload spikes (up to 5,000 hires in 12 weeks, layered on top of an estimated 8,000 to 9,000 hires per year across Network Operations, Project Delivery and Early Careers) with a permanent TA team historically as small as around 28 people plus a large casual surge workforce whose own churn and onboarding adds further load, all while Post30 simultaneously demands "eliminate, simplify, automate" thinking applied across the business, including implicitly their own function. Source: Ben Roberts LinkedIn bio (historical evidence of scale), WSWS Post30 article, pulled 14 July 2026. Flag: exact current TA team size is UNKNOWN and may have changed since Roberts' tenure; used here as directional evidence of scale, not a confirmed current fact.
- **Root Cause / Career Pain:** Post30 is explicitly an AI and automation first mandate direct from the CEO ("eliminate," "simplify," or "automate" every process, algorithmic delivery tracking, the Rendr AI acquisition). Any People & Culture leader still running frontline hiring through a base ATS with manual downstream screening is structurally out of step with the direction their own CEO has just told the workforce and, separately, the Senate, the company is going. Susan Davies is one of the more senior and highly remunerated people leaders in the ANZ market (approximately $1.66 million total remuneration) with direct visibility to a board and two federal Shareholder Ministers; failing to demonstrate a modernised, AI enabled hiring capability while the CEO has publicly staked the company's next four years on exactly that kind of transformation is a live reputational and career exposure.
