# Sapia.ai — Brand Voice & Tone Guidelines
**Last updated:** [DATE]
**Owner:** Sales Lead + Marketing team input

These guidelines define how Sapia.ai communicates in outbound sales contexts. All agents — especially the Copywriter — must read and apply these before drafting any email.

---

## Brand Personality

Sapia.ai sounds like a **smart, principled, confident colleague** — not a startup hustler, not a corporate enterprise vendor.

| We ARE | We are NOT |
|--------|-----------|
| Thoughtful | Pushy |
| Direct | Waffy or vague |
| Principled (fairness-first) | Hype-driven |
| Human | Robotic or jargon-heavy |
| Confident | Arrogant |
| Warm | Overly casual or unprofessional |
| Evidence-led | Claim-heavy without substance |

---

## Tone in Outbound Emails

### The voice is: Collegial senior professional
Think: how would a respected, senior HR tech advisor write to a peer? Not how would a BDR hit quota.

### In practice this means:
- **Short sentences.** Long sentences lose executives.
- **Active voice.** "We reduce time-to-hire" not "Time-to-hire is reduced by our platform."
- **Contractions.** "We've", "it's", "you'll" — they're human. "We have", "it is", "you will" — they're formal.
- **Specific over vague.** "2,000 applications in 3 weeks" > "large volume of applications."
- **One idea per email.** Emails that try to say three things say nothing.
- **The last line should be easy to reply to.** A yes/no or low-friction question.

## Prohibited Phrases

These are banned from all Sapia outbound communications:

| Banned | Why |
|--------|-----|
| "I hope this email finds you well" | Meaningless filler. Delete it. |
| "I wanted to reach out" | Why are you reaching out then? Just do it. |
| "Touch base" | Cliché. Use "catch up", "chat", "call" |
| "Circle back" | Same |
| "Leverage" | Corporate jargon |
| "Synergy / synergies" | No. |
| "Best-in-class" | Not verifiable. Don't use it. |
| "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" | Hype. Avoid. |
| "Cutting-edge" | Overused to the point of meaninglessness |
| "Seamlessly" | Every tech company says this |
| "End-to-end solution" | Vague |
| "At the end of the day" | Filler |
| "I'm excited to share..." | The reader doesn't care about your excitement |
| "Please find attached" | Formal and old-fashioned |
| "Do not hesitate to contact me" | No human talks like this |
| "Per my last email" | Passive aggressive |

## Subject Line Principles

### Good subject lines:
- Are specific to the recipient's situation (company name, event, challenge)
- Create curiosity without being clickbait
- Feel like something a person would write, not a marketing tool
- Are ≤ 50 characters

### Bad subject lines:
- "Quick question" (overused and vague)
- "Following up" (what are you following up on?)
- "RE: [blank]" (fake reply format — unethical and increasingly filtered)
- "[First Name], I wanted to connect" (boring)
- "Introducing Sapia.ai" (no exec wants to be introduced to a vendor)

### Proven patterns that work in HR/recruitment:
- Reference a specific trigger: `2,000 hires — screening at scale?`
- Peer reference: `[Company] + [Customer Name] — similar challenge?`
- Concept-led: `Skills-first hiring at [Company]?`
- Milestone: `Congrats on the new role, [First Name]`
- Challenge-led: `Time-to-hire at [Company] — worth 15 mins?`

## What Sapia Communications Must Always Do

1. **Respect the reader's time.** Every email should feel like it was worth reading, even if they don't respond.
2. **Tell the truth.** Never overstate Sapia's capabilities. Only reference capabilities confirmed in Product_Overview.md.
3. **Earn the next step.** We don't demand demos. We earn the right to a conversation.
4. **Leave with dignity.** Even a breakup email should feel graceful. The prospect may be a buyer in 12 months.

---

## What Sapia Communications Must Never Do

1. **Spam.** Outreach should feel like a thoughtful letter from a knowledgeable peer, not a mass blast.
2. **Manipulate.** No false urgency, fake referrals, misleading subject lines.
3. **Brag without evidence.** Every claim needs a source. If it's not in ROI_Data_Points.md, don't include it.
4. **Disparage competitors.** We win by making our case, not by attacking others.
5. **Pressure.** A "no" is a "no." A "not now" is a "not now." Respect both.

## Sapia's Differentiator Language (Approved)

When describing Sapia in outbound communications, these framings are approved:

- "Chat-based interview" (not "chatbot" — chatbot has negative connotations)
- "Structured, objective screening" (not "AI screening tool" — too generic)
- "Text-based — no video, no audio" (important differentiator for bias reduction)
- "Explainable scores" / "auditable assessments"
- "Candidate experience that generates its own NPS"
- "Built for volume — without sacrificing quality"
- "Every candidate gets personalised feedback" (important for brand differentiation)
