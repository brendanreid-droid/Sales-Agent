# Sapia.ai — Stakeholder Persona Maps
**Last updated:** July 2026
**Owner:** Sales Lead

These maps define what each target persona cares about, what their day looks like, and how to talk to them. The Copywriter and Research Analyst must read this before crafting email hooks.

---

## Persona 1: CHRO / Chief People Officer / Head of People & Culture

### Their World
- Reports to CEO, sits on executive committee
- Accountable for board-level people metrics: diversity outcomes, employee satisfaction, cost-per-hire, time-to-productivity, and voluntary turnover reduction
- Receives pressure from the board on DEI targets and compliance
- Worried about: hiring risk, brand reputation, talent retention, and whether their HR tech stack is future-fit to reduce employee churn

### Their Pain Points
- CV screening is subjective — no two recruiters screen the same way, leading to inconsistent hires and high 12-month turnover
- Hard to prove the ROI of HR tech investment at board level
- DEI goals require structural change, not just goodwill
- High employee attrition creates massive invisible costs (constant backfilling, re-hiring fees, lost retraining investments) and ruins organisational morale
- Recruiter fatigue and burnout from being stuck in a constant, reactive backfilling loop

### What Gets Their Attention
- Measurable reduction in employee turnover within the first 12 months
- Board-level outcomes: diversity percentages, cost per hire, time to productivity, and employee lifetime value (retention)
- Hard ROI from data-driven decision-making (unveiling the invisible cost savings of less retraining and re-hiring)
- Risk language: "bias exposure", "compliance", "explainable AI"
- peer references: what other CHROs at similar companies have done to reduce churn

### Email Hook Ideas
- Recent DEI report or diversity commitment they made publicly
- New CHRO appointment hook: "You're [N] months in — what's on your radar for the first-mile of hiring and retention?"
- Regulatory context: AI in hiring regulation coming to their region/sector
- Turnover statistics in their sector and the EBITDA impact of reducing it

### Do NOT Say
- "How are you finding the current candidate market?" (too generic)
- "Our platform has the following features..." (features bore CHROs)
- Anything that sounds like a HR tech sales pitch, not a strategic conversation

## Persona 2: Head of Talent Acquisition / TA Director / Head of Business Partnering / Recruitment

### Their World
- Manages the TA function and Recruitment Business Partnering end-to-end
- Owns the ATS, recruiter productivity, candidate experience metrics, hiring manager/business unit alignment, and recruiter burnout prevention
- Their team is often overloaded — high req load, high application volumes, under-resourced
- Under pressure to align recruitment capacity with business unit volume spikes and maintain quality candidate experience without increasing HR headcount

### Pain Points
- Recruiters spend >60% of their week on manual screening, reading CVs, and playing phone tag. This repetitive admin leads to high recruiter fatigue and burnout.
- Inconsistency: different recruiters shortlist differently for the same role, making hiring manager feedback volatile
- Candidate drop-off: lengthy or impersonal screening drives strong candidates to drop
- ATS does not help with screening quality — it just receives applications, dumping admin on the recruiters
- Seasonal peaks create impossible spikes in workload, driving recruiters to leave

### What Gets Their Attention
- Saving >60% of their recruiters' work week by automating CV reading, phone screening, and phone tag
- Reclaiming time to focus on high-value, business-critical tasks: partnering with business units, taking on extra requisitions, and spending quality time with top-fit candidates
- Reductions in recruiter fatigue and attrition
- Shortlist quality improvement (not just speed — quality and retention potential matter)
- Candidate experience data — NPS scores, completion rates

### Email Hook Ideas
- Volume of open roles visible on their careers page right now and recruiter capacity to fill them
- Seasonal hiring period coming up
- Industry hiring campaign they've announced
- New role they're hiring for that suggests internal TA growth

### Do NOT Say
- Anything that implies their recruiters can be replaced — they will shut down
- "AI replaces recruiters" — never. Sapia optimises their week so they can do actual high-value human partnering.
- Overly technical product descriptions

## Persona 3: COO / Chief Operating Officer (Frontline-heavy industries)

### Their World
- Owns headcount across operations — frontline stores, logistics, contact centres
- Every unfilled role is a direct cost (lost productivity, overtime, customer impact)
- Peak seasons are existential — if they cannot ramp headcount in time, the business suffers
- Focuses on operational efficiency and margins: high turnover directly degrades customer service and store profitability

### Their Pain Points
- Time to fill frontline roles is too long — the business runs lean and vacancies hurt
- Recruiting for frontline at scale is expensive and inconsistent, with no data-driven way to predict which hires will stay
- High turnover means they are stuck in a constant hiring loop, leading to massive retraining costs, low team morale, and recruitment waste
- HR and traditional screening are too slow for operational timelines

### What Gets Their Attention
- Meaningful and measurable reduction in employee turnover within the first 12 months (direct bottom-line margin savings)
- Business impact language: cost of vacancy, productivity per role, customer service impact, and reduction in retraining/re-hiring costs
- Speed: "shortlist in 24 hours vs. 3 weeks"
- Scale: "process 5,000 applications in 48 hours during peak"
- Retention: "better-fit hires = lower 90-day attrition and higher customer NPS"

### Email Hook Ideas
- Peak trading period coming up (Black Friday, Christmas, Easter)
- Expansion announcement (new stores, new markets)
- High turnover in their sector being reported publicly, and the hidden cost of retraining frontline staff

### Do NOT Say
- HR jargon: "candidate experience", "talent strategy"
- Anything that sounds like an HR conversation — speak operations, staffing stability, and margins

## Persona 4: Head of People Systems (People Systems Manager/Director) & HR Tech Leader

### Their World
- Owns HR systems, people data, analytics, and workforce planning
- Evaluates all HR tech through the lens of: integration, data integrity, compliance, vendor risk
- Asks the hard questions about AI models, data privacy, bias testing, and auditability

### Their Pain Points
- AI in hiring is a compliance minefield — they need explainable, auditable models
- Integration with existing stack is always complex and often fails
- Data privacy and candidate consent are under-scrutiny
- Many AI hiring vendors have been burned for bias — they're cautious

### What Gets Their Attention
- Technical credibility: text-only, no demographic proxies, explainable scores
- Integration story: what ATS/HRIS Sapia integrates with natively
- Compliance: relevant regulations (EU AI Act, local equivalents) and how Sapia addresses them
- Data governance: where candidate data goes, how long it's retained

### Email Hook Ideas
- AI in hiring regulation news relevant to their region
- A compliance or bias story in their sector (careful how this is framed)
- ATS migration or upgrade they've announced

### Do NOT Say
- Generic AI claims — they will immediately see through it
- Anything unverified about compliance — huge risk
- "Cutting-edge AI" or "revolutionary machine learning" — they'll laugh

## Persona 5: CEO / Managing Director / Founder

### Their World
- Focuses on enterprise growth, operational margins (EBITDA), market share, and brand equity
- Sits under intense pressure to meet ESG/DEI targets from public markets, boards, and investors
- Worried about scale blockages—when open frontline or technical roles cannot be filled, it directly limits top-line revenue growth
- Focused on capital efficiency: optimising HR/TA headcount and reducing bottom-line waste

### Their Pain Points
- High operational spend on talent recruitment (recruiter headcount, agencies, advertising) without visible proof of hire quality or retention outcomes
- Frontline staff attrition (retail, hospitality) that damages customer experience and directly leads to lost sales and constant re-hiring/retraining costs
- Brand risk and legal exposure from subjective, non-auditable candidate screening practices (risk of bias claims)
- Recruiter burnout and high TA team turnover due to repetitive, manual administrative tasks

### What Gets Their Attention
- Measurable reduction in voluntary turnover within the first 12 months, locking in massive invisible cost savings (retraining, backfilling, team morale)
- Process scaling: saving >60% of their TA team's week from CV reading and phone screening, allowing the team to focus on high-value business-critical tasks (business partnering, strategic sourcing)
- Margin improvements: reducing cost-of-hire, increasing time-to-productivity, and optimising EBITDA
- Strategic Risk Mitigation: bias-free, audited, compliant AI hiring processes
- High-level peer proof: "We helped Woolworths secure their frontline talent pipeline while reducing attrition by X%"

### Email Hook Ideas
- Corporate expansion plans, new store/branch openings, or regional launches
- Annual reports referencing margins, EBITDA growth targets, or ESG/DEI metrics
- Press articles quoting the CEO's growth or customer experience goals

### Do NOT Say
- Sourcing tactics or recruiter task management metrics (keeps it too low-level)
- Simple feature lists or software screens—focus strictly on operational and brand outcomes

## Persona 6: Head of People Services / People Services Manager / People Services Director

### Their World
- Owns the HR shared services function — the operational engine that delivers HR processes at scale across the business
- Responsible for SLA delivery: time-to-fill, time-to-offer, onboarding completion, and first-day readiness
- Sits between the strategic HR/CHRO layer and the frontline business units, translating people strategy into process execution
- Common in large organisations (1,000+ employees) with a centralised HR operating model — especially retail, healthcare, logistics, financial services, and the public sector
- May also hold titles such as: Director of People Operations, Head of HR Operations, People Operations Manager, or VP People Services

### Their Pain Points
- Accountable for SLAs that are constantly missed due to manual, high-volume screening backlogs
- Their team absorbs the overflow from TA — reference checks, onboarding coordination, compliance paperwork — on top of screening volume
- Inconsistency in screening quality across different hiring managers or business units means re-work and complaints
- Difficult to report upward on service delivery metrics when the data is fragmented across spreadsheets and email threads
- Peak hiring periods (seasonal ramps, new site openings) break their team's capacity without warning
- High staff turnover within the People Services team itself due to admin overload and repetitive manual work

### What Gets Their Attention
- Measurable SLA improvements: time-to-shortlist, time-to-offer, percentage of roles filled within target
- Reducing manual workload for their team: hours saved per recruiter/coordinator per week
- Standardisation: a consistent, auditable screening process across all business units and hiring managers
- Onboarding readiness: better-screened candidates who are more likely to show up, stay, and complete probation
- Dashboard and reporting capability: real-time visibility into funnel metrics they can present to the CHRO

### Email Hook Ideas
- Volume of open roles on their careers page right now vs. their team's visible capacity
- A recent public announcement about HR service transformation or shared services restructure
- Sector-specific SLA benchmarks: "Healthcare people services teams are averaging 28 days to shortlist — here’s how some are getting it to 7"
- New site opening, merger integration, or rapid headcount expansion that will stress their service delivery model

### Do NOT Say
- Strategic or board-level language only — they care about delivery, not vision
- "Candidate experience" as the only hook — they care equally about internal team experience and SLA compliance
- Anything that implies their team is replaceable — frame Sapia as the tool that lets their people do the job they were hired for
- Overly technical product descriptions — they want process outcomes, not AI architecture
