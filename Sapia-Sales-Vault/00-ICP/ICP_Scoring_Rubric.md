# Sapia.ai — ICP Scoring Rubric
**Last updated:** [DATE]
**Owner:** ICP Scorer Agent + Sales Lead (calibrate after each 4-week cycle)

## Scoring Overview

| Dimension | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| 1. Company Size | 20% |
| 2. Industry Fit | 25% |
| 3. Hiring Volume Signal | 20% |
| 4. DEI/Equity Mandate | 15% |
| 5. ATS/HRIS Tech Stack | 10% |
| 6. Budget/Growth Signals | 10% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

**Tier Thresholds:**
- 75–100 → Tier A (Priority this week)
- 50–74 → Tier B (Queue — next 2 weeks)
- 30–49 → Tier C (Monitor 90 days)
- 0–29 → Exclude

---

## Dimension 1: Company Size (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Headcount | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 10 | >5,000 | Peak sweet spot — high volume, budget, enterprise procurement |
| 8 | 2,000–5,000 | Strong fit — meaningful scale |
| 6 | 1,000–2,000 | Viable — mid-market enterprise |
| 0 | <1,000 | Excluded — too small (no scale need) |

---

## Dimension 2: Industry Fit (Weight: 25%)

| Score | Industry | Rationale |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 10 | Retail, Hospitality/F&B, Telco, Public Sector | Core ICP — proven ROI, high volume hiring, DEI mandates |
| 8 | Banking, Healthcare, Logistics, Financial Services (frontline heavy) | Strong adjacent — high-volume branch/frontline hiring |
| 5 | Professional Services, Media, FMCG | Possible but lower hiring volume signal |
| 2 | Tech/SaaS, Consulting | Low hiring volume in target roles |
| 0 | Academic institutions, NGOs, pure Government | Long procurement, wrong procurement, or no fit |

---

## Dimension 3: Hiring Volume Signal (Weight: 20%)

| Score | Evidence | Sources |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 10 | Ideal volume (1,000+ hires/year) — active hiring campaigns, high-volume seasonal patterns | Press, LinkedIn jobs, annual report |
| 7 | Acceptable volume (400-1,000 hires/year) — steady open roles, moderate hiring activity | LinkedIn Jobs, Lusha, company website |
| 0 | Excluded volume (<400 hires/year) — low hiring volume, hiring freezes, or redundancies | News, company announcements |

---

## Dimension 4: DEI/Equity Mandate Evidence (Weight: 15%)

| Score | Evidence | Sources |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 10 | Formal DEI program with public targets, skills-based hiring policy, dedicated DEI leadership role | CSR/ESG reports, job posts, website |
| 7 | ESG reporting includes diversity metrics, public DEI commitments | Annual reports, press releases |
| 4 | Standard EEO statements, or no public evidence of DEI focus (never exclude, they still get full value from speed, efficiency, and turnover ROI) | Job posts, company website |

---

## Dimension 5: ATS/HRIS Tech Stack (Weight: 10%)

| Score | Stack | Rationale |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| 10 | SAP SuccessFactors, PageUp, SmartRecruiters, Greenhouse, Tribepad, iCIMS, eArcu, TeamTailor, Avature, LiveHire, Dayforce, Workday, Oracle HCM | Native integration — low implementation friction |
| 7 | Lever, Jobvite, legacy or modern ATS not in Tier 10 | Modern ATS — good integration potential |
| 5 | Unknown / unconfirmed | Do not penalise — score at midpoint |
| 3 | Taleo legacy, custom or niche ATS | Integration needs evaluation |
| 1 | No ATS — spreadsheet/manual process | High implementation complexity |

---

## Dimension 6: Budget/Growth Signals (Weight: 10%)

| Score | Evidence | Sources |
|-------|----------|---------|
| 10 | Funding round (Series B+), IPO, M&A activity, new geography launch, major expansion or public digital/tech/AI transformation announcements | Crunchbase, press, company announcements |
| 7 | Strong revenue growth publicly reported, new business division, new product line | Annual report, press |
| 4 | Stable company, no specific growth signals found | — |
| 1 | Cost-cutting, restructuring, negative revenue news | News |
| 0 | Confirmed budget freeze, company in distress | News, Glassdoor signals |
