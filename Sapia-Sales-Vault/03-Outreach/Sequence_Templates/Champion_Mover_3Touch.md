# Champion Mover 3-Touch Sequence
**For:** Stakeholders who previously worked at a Sapia customer or known prospect and have moved to a new company
**Channel:** SalesLoft cadence (high priority — deploy within 48hrs of detection)
**Timing:** Touch 1 → T+5 days → T+10 days

---

## Context
This is the warmest outbound signal in the entire system. A champion mover:
- Already knows Sapia (or the problem Sapia solves)
- Has experienced the value directly
- Is starting fresh at a new company where they likely have influence and budget appetite
- Is in their honeymoon period — looking to make early impact

The tone is collegial and warm. You have implicit shared context. Don't waste it with a generic email.

**CRITICAL: Do not make the email feel surveillance-y.** Frame the new job discovery naturally.

---

## Touch 1 — The Warm Congratulation + Bridge
**Goal:** Acknowledge the move genuinely. Pivot naturally to the new company's context and Sapia's relevance.
**Timing:** Within 48 hours of detection (priority flag from Sales Director)
**Word count:** ≤ 110 words
**Subject line approach:** Personal — use their first name or "congrats on the new role"

**Template skeleton:**
```
Subject: Congrats on the move, [First Name]

[First Name],

Saw the news about your move to [New Company] — congratulations. 

[1 sentence acknowledging the new company and what they're known for — 
or a genuine observation about the challenge they face in that role at that company]

[1–2 sentences: natural bridge. NOT "So now that you're there, can we talk about Sapia again?"
Instead: "Curious whether [challenge Sapia solves] is already on your list at [New Company].
It usually comes up quickly in [their industry/size/context]."]

[CTA: Would a catch-up be worth it? — keep it casual, not corporate]

[Sender name]
```

**Example (for a TA Director who moved from a retailer to a new one):**
```
Subject: Congrats on the new role, Sarah

Sarah,

Saw you've joined [New Company] — exciting move. A great brand with what I'd imagine is a seriously busy TA function given the expansion they've been on.

Given your background, I'm guessing hiring at scale is going to be front of mind pretty quickly. 
Would love to reconnect and hear what you're thinking — and share what we've been doing with a few 
of your peers in the sector.

Worth a catch-up?

[Sender name]
```

---

## Touch 2 — The Peer Reference
**Goal:** If Touch 1 didn't land, provide a relevant reference point from their new company's industry.
**Timing:** 5 business days after Touch 1 (if no reply)
**Word count:** ≤ 90 words
**Subject line:** Different from Touch 1 — try a company/sector reference

**Template skeleton:**
```
Subject: [New Company] + [Sapia customer in same sector]?

[First Name] — following up from last week.

[1 sentence: reference what a peer company (Sapia customer) in their new industry is doing]

[1 sentence: bridge to their new context]

[CTA: same low-friction ask]

[Sender name]
```

---

## Touch 3 — Graceful Breakup
**Goal:** Close the sequence warmly. They know Sapia. They know us. Leave the door open — wide.
**Timing:** 10 days after Touch 2 (if no reply)
**Word count:** ≤ 60 words
**Subject line:** "Last one from me" or similar, personal

**Template skeleton:**
```
Subject: Last note, [First Name]

[First Name] — I'll leave you to it.

You know what we do and where we are. 
When the time's right at [New Company], I'd love to reconnect.

Good luck with the new chapter — sounds like an exciting one.

[Sender name]
```

---

## Sequence Rules
- Touch 1 must be sent within 48 hours. This is the whole point of monitoring job moves.
- Never open with "I was notified you changed jobs" or reference LinkedIn tracking.
- Reference the shared history subtly — not as a sales hook, as genuine context.
- This sequence does NOT become a cold sequence if no response. Respect the relationship.
- Log outcome in `05-Signals/Job_Movers_Log.md` regardless of result.
