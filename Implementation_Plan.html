Sapia.ai — AI Sales Agent Team: Implementation Plan

Autonomous Outbound GTM Lead Generation Platform on Claude Desktop

Overview

This plan describes a multi-agent orchestration system running inside Claude Desktop (Team/Enterprise plan) that automates the research, qualification, personalisation, and outreach lifecycle for Sapia.ai's outbound GTM motion. All agents share a common Foundational Knowledge Vault and communicate through a Sales Director orchestrator that governs cadence, approval gates, and output quality.

The system is designed to be: - Human-in-the-loop at the critical "send" moment — no emails go out without review - Cadence-controlled — 2–3 outreach batches per week, not all-day noise - Shareable — built as Claude Projects so all peers inherit the exact same setup - Iterative — the agents feed learnings back into the knowledge vault over time

2. Agent Roster — System Prompts & Responsibilities

🎯 Agent 0: Sales Director (Orchestrator)

Claude Project Name: SAPIA-SD — Sales Director

Role: The conductor. Plans the weekly outreach plan, routes work to specialist agents, sets priorities from signal inputs (newsletter engagement, job moves, new lookalikes), reviews all outputs against the 8-Point Copywriting Audit before surfacing a morning digest to the human SDR for final approval.

Key Behaviours: - Runs a Monday planning session to assign the week's target accounts and outreach slots - Enforces the 2–3 send-days-per-week cadence by queuing and scheduling batches - Audits Copywriter drafts against the 8-Point Copywriting Checklist (jargon, dashes, openers, CTAs) - Produces a "Morning Digest" each send morning: a Slack-ready summary of all drafted emails ready for human approval - Flags anomalies: replies, OOOs, negative signals - Feeds approved/rejected email outcomes back to the Copywriter for learning

🔍 Agent 1: Company & Stakeholder Research Analyst

Claude Project Name: SAPIA-RA — Research Analyst

MCP Connections: HubSpot, Lusha, LinkedIn Sales Nav*, Brave Search

Role: Deep research on target accounts and stakeholders. Also performs lookalike discovery — identifying companies and individuals that match the ICP but are not yet in the database.

Key Behaviours: - For each target account: industry, size, recent news, tech stack, hiring activity, DEI commitments, ATS/HRIS in use, leadership team - Identifies key stakeholders: CHRO, Head of Talent Acquisition, CPO, HR Director, COO - Runs lookalike scans using Lusha MCP to find companies matching ICP firmographics (industry, headcount, ATS stack, hiring volume signals) - Tracks LinkedIn job moves: stakeholders from existing customers who have moved to new companies (triggers Champion Mover workflow) - Flags HubSpot newsletter openers who match ICP stakeholder profiles (triggers Warm Engagement workflow) - Outputs a structured research brief per company in Markdown

📊 Agent 2: ICP Scorer

Claude Project Name: SAPIA-ICP — ICP Scorer

MCP Connections: HubSpot, Lusha

Role: Objectively scores every prospect company and stakeholder against the Sapia.ai ICP rubric. Prevents wasted outreach on poor-fit accounts.

Scoring Rubric (to be refined with your team):

Dimension Weight Signal
Company size (500–50k employees) 20% Lusha / LinkedIn
Industry fit (Retail/Hosp/Telco/Public Sector) 25% Research brief
Hiring volume (high-volume, continuous) 20% LinkedIn jobs, news
DEI mandate evidence 15% News, website, ESG reports
ATS/HRIS in use (SAP SF, Workday, etc.) 10% Lusha, BuiltWith
Budget signals (funding round, expansion) 10% Press, Crunchbase

Output: ICP Score (0–100), Tier (A/B/C), and a one-line rationale per company. Only Tier A and B accounts proceed to outreach.

✍️ Agent 3: Copywriter / Voice-Matched Writer

Claude Project Name: SAPIA-CW — Copywriter

MCP Connections: Gong, Filesystem

Role: Drafts all outbound emails. Voice-matches to the individual sender. Calibrates using personal voice profiles + Gong transcripts to build a Customer Language Library. Audits drafts against a strict 8-point copywriting checklist and applies Permissionless Value Promise (PVP) CTAs.

Key Behaviours: - Voice Calibration Phase: Builds customer language libraries from best email winners and Gong calls (filtered strictly by sender's email) before drafting. - Enforces 8-Point Copywriting Audit (no em dashes, no rhetorical hooks, no generic flattery, no "I" openers, no jargon, strict length limits). - Implements Permissionless Value Promise (PVP) CTAs (Insight, Benchmark, Playbook, Diagnostic Question) replacing meeting pitches. - Spaces email sequences on a high-converting rhythm: Day 0 (Opener) / Day 3 (Value benchmark) / Day 7 (Diagnostic question) / Day 12 (Breakup / soft resource). - Handles three email archetypes: - Cold outreach (standard ICP prospect) - Warm engagement (newsletter opener who is an ICP stakeholder) - Champion mover (ex-customer stakeholder at new company) - Subject lines ≤ 6 words (sentence case, lowercase, no clickbait), body ≤ 120 words for Email 1, ≤ 150 words for follow-ups.

🏹 Agent 4: SDR / Prospect Hunter

Claude Project Name: SAPIA-PH — Prospect Hunter

MCP Connections: Lusha, LinkedIn Sales Nav*, SalesLoft

Role: The data acquisition and pipeline loading agent. Finds contacts, enriches records, de-dupes against SalesLoft, and stages approved prospects into SalesLoft cadences.

Key Behaviours: - Takes the Research Analyst's lookalike list and enriches each company with verified email and LinkedIn URL via Lusha MCP (strictly no phone number lookups to protect credits) - Checks SalesLoft for existing contact records to avoid re-prospecting accounts already in an active cadence - Stages contacts in SalesLoft (does not auto-enrol in a cadence — human confirmation required) - Tracks LinkedIn Sales Nav job-move alerts for the Champion Mover workflow - Outputs a weekly "Prospect Queue" report with regional tagging (APAC / MENA / UK-EU-US)

💬 Agent 5: Reply Handler & Meeting Qualifier

Claude Project Name: SAPIA-RH — Reply Handler

MCP Connections: SalesLoft, Slack, Filesystem

Role: Monitors replies to outbound sequences using SalesLoft's native reply tracking. Classifies intent, drafts a suggested reply, and qualifies meetings. Escalates positives immediately to the human via Slack — the primary approval and notification channel.

Key Behaviours: - Classifies replies: Positive / Referral / Objection / OOO / Unsubscribe / Not interested - For positives: drafts a reply proposing meeting time, updates SalesLoft opportunity stage, sends immediate Slack DM alert to the rep with the full draft - For objections: drafts a soft objection-handle reply using the vault's objection library, posts to Slack for rep approval - For OOOs: flags for follow-up on the return date in SalesLoft - For unsubscribes: immediately removes from SalesLoft cadence and logs the action - After a confirmed meeting: drafts a meeting brief for the AE/rep based on the trigger hook and reply context

3a. HubSpot Regional Territory Configuration

The team operates across three distinct territories. Each rep's agent team queries HubSpot filtered to their region, ensuring newsletter engagement signals are routed to the correct rep and there is no cross-territory duplication.

🇬🇧 UK UK + EU + US Company country IN: United Kingdom, EU member states, United States, Canada UK-EU-US-Pipeline

How Territory Filtering Works

HubSpot MCP queries — each rep's Research Analyst agent is instructed to include a territory filter in every HubSpot query. Example instruction in the APAC Research Analyst system prompt:

When querying HubSpot for newsletter engagers, always filter by:
  contact.company_country IN [Australia, New Zealand, Singapore, Japan, 
  South Korea, India, Philippines, Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand, Vietnam]
Do not return contacts from other regions — they are managed by separate reps.

Lusha lookalike scans — each rep's Prospect Hunter filters by geography: - APAC rep: company HQ in Asia-Pacific region - MENA rep: company HQ in Middle East, North Africa, sub-Saharan Africa - UK rep: company HQ in United Kingdom, Europe, North America

LinkedIn Sales Nav CSV — the monthly export should be pre-filtered by territory before being shared with each rep. Alternatively, each rep exports only their region's saved lists from Sales Navigator.

SalesLoft cadences — set up three parallel cadence groups, one per territory, so send-day timing respects local time zones: - APAC: send at 8–9am AEST (Tue/Thu) - MENA: send at 8–9am GST (Tue/Thu) - UK/EU/US: send at 8–9am GMT/BST (Tue/Thu)

5. Volume & Cost Management

Design principle: Start small, measure everything, scale on evidence. The system is built to enforce these limits automatically — agents will decline requests that exceed them and explain why.

Starting Volume (Phase 1–2: Weeks 1–4)

Metric Limit Reason
Contacts per send session 100 Must be from 100 different companies (1 contact per company per batch) to prevent spamming multiple contacts at the same company simultaneously.
Send sessions per week per rep 2 (Tue + Thu) 200 emails/week — enough signal to learn from, not enough to saturate
Total emails/week per rep 200 Starting volume across both send days
Total emails/week (3 reps) ~600 Manageable for reply monitoring and qualification
Lusha enrichment per week per rep 200 contacts max Enriches the 200 weekly send-batch — strictly limited to email-only (200 credits total).
Company research briefs per session 10 max Prevents runaway token usage in a single agent session
Prospect Hunter discovery scan 50 accounts per run Caps token usage; produces more than enough candidates for the batch

Scaling Protocol — Evidence-Gated

Volume only increases when the data justifies it. The Sales Director reviews these metrics every Friday and posts a scaling recommendation to #sapia-performance.

Scale UP (increase batch to 120 or add a 3rd send day) when ALL of the following are true for 2 consecutive weeks: - Open rate > 35% - Reply rate > 5% - Zero spam complaints or deliverability warnings - Meeting rate > 1% of sends - Rep confirms they can handle the increased reply volume

Hold (keep at current volume) when: - Open rate 25–35% and reply rate 3–5% — system is working, let it compound - Less than 2 weeks of data — insufficient to make a decision

Scale DOWN (reduce to 50 per batch or drop to 1 send day) if ANY of the following appear: - Open rate < 20% for 2+ consecutive weeks - Deliverability warnings in SalesLoft - Reply rate < 2% - Domain flagged as spam by any major mail provider - Rep is unable to action positive replies within the same business day

Do not scale above 150 contacts per batch until the system has been running for at least 8 weeks and has demonstrated sustained performance above all Scale UP thresholds.

Lusha Credit Management

Lusha charges per enrichment (email verification, phone, firmographic data). To avoid wasting credits on low-quality contacts:

Total budget pool: 240,000 Lusha credits. Credit consumption rule: 1 email address = 1 credit. Phone numbers cost an extra 6 credits. Always scrape emails only. Scrapes of phone numbers are strictly prohibited.

| Rule | Detail | |---|---|---| | Score before enrich | ICP Scorer must tier a company as A or B before the Prospect Hunter spends any Lusha credits on contact enrichment | | Emails only (No phones) | Configure the Lusha MCP query to scrape email addresses only. Do not request phone numbers or dialers. If a phone number is scraped, it consumes 6x extra credits. | | No bulk enrichment | Prospect Hunter enriches contacts in batches of 25 — never the full discovery list at once | | Weekly credit alert | If estimated credits for the week exceed 200 per rep (600 total), Sales Director posts a warning to #sapia-alerts before proceeding | | No re-enrichment | Do not re-enrich contacts already in SalesLoft — check first, enrich only net-new | | Skip database gaps | If Lusha returns no email match for a contact, do not purchase a phone credit as a fallback — flag for manual research instead |

Claude Token Management

Each agent session consumes Claude tokens. Long research briefs, large discovery scans, and multi-email drafting are the main cost drivers. The following session limits are hard limits enforced by the Sales Director:

Session Hard Limit Why
Prospect Hunter discovery scan 50 accounts Beyond this, a single session risks hitting context limits
Company research briefs (per session) 10 companies Deep briefs are token-heavy; batch and run across multiple sessions if needed
Email drafting per session 25 emails Run four Copywriter sessions for a 100-contact batch (25 + 25 + 25 + 25) — better quality, no context overflow
ICP scoring per session 50 contacts Sufficient for a week's pipeline
Reply Handler per session Process all replies — no limit Replies are short; this is low token cost

If you're approaching context limits mid-session: stop, save progress to the vault (Filesystem MCP), start a fresh session, and continue. The Sales Director is responsible for managing this pipeline across sessions.

    → Prospect Hunter: enriches contacts, checks SalesLoft for existing records
    → Stages contacts in SalesLoft as "Pending Approval"
    → Sales Director: posts weekly Prospect Queue to Slack for rep review

7. Human-in-the-Loop Design Principles

[!IMPORTANT] The system is deliberately designed so no email is ever sent automatically. Every outreach batch requires explicit human approval. This protects your sender reputation and ensures quality control.

Prospect Approval New lookalike accounts staged in SalesLoft (max 200/week) Rep reviews weekly Prospect Queue posted to Slack; approves or declines
Morning Digest Approval All drafted emails posted to Slack by 8am send-day (cap: 100) Rep reads in Slack, replies APPROVE / HOLD / [number + edit note]
Batch Override Request Rep requests >100 contacts in a session Sales Director declines, explains guardrail, queues excess for next session
Lusha Credit Warning Estimated weekly spend exceeds 200 credits per rep Rep confirms explicit override before Prospect Hunter continues enrichment
Scaling Recommendation Friday — Sales Director posts scale-up/hold/scale-down recommendation Rep confirms or overrides the scaling decision
Positive Reply Escalation Slack DM alert within 5 min with drafted response Rep reviews draft in Slack, sends or edits
Objection Response Draft posted to Slack for review Rep approves or rewrites before sending
Champion Mover Alert Slack DM flagged 🔴 PRIORITY — draft ready Rep must approve within 48hrs to capture the momentum window
Unsubscribe Auto-actioned (removed from cadence) No action required — logged automatically

🛡️ Spam & Overlap Protection Controls

To prevent spamming the same contacts or duplicate outreach to the same companies: - 90-Day Cooldown: Prospect Hunter checks SalesLoft and Outreach_History_Log.csv. Any contact emailed in the last 90 days is automatically skipped. - 1 Contact Per Company Per Batch: Outreach batches are restricted to 1 contact per company at a time. Multi-stakeholder targets are staggered across future send sessions.

8. Slack as the Approval & Notification Hub

Slack is the primary interface between the agent team and the human reps. Once the weekly run is started in Claude Desktop, the rep manages all approvals and edits directly inside Slack.

How the Slack Approval Loop Works

  1. Agent Posts: Sales Director generates the weekly plan or Morning Digest and posts it directly to your designated regional Slack channel.
  2. Rep Actions in Slack: You review the content on your phone/desktop and reply in Slack (e.g. typing APPROVE or HOLD or 4 EDIT: make hook shorter).
  3. Agent Reads & Executes: When you run the Claude session and say "Check Slack approvals and run", the Sales Director calls the Slack MCP tool to fetch your reply, modifies the drafts based on your Slack input, and tells the Prospect Hunter to stage them in SalesLoft.

9. Claude Projects Setup — Team Sharing

Each agent is its own Claude Project within the Team workspace. All projects reference the shared Knowledge Vault. Since the team operates across three regions, each rep runs their own instance of the agent Projects — pre-configured with their territory filter and connected to their own Slack channel.

Setup Steps for Each Agent Project:

  1. In Claude Desktop → New Project
  2. Name it per the naming convention: SAPIA-[REGION]-[AGENT] (e.g., SAPIA-APAC-SD)
  3. Upload the relevant Vault documents + the rep's regional territory file
  4. Paste the agent's system prompt (with territory filter added)
  5. Connect the relevant MCP servers for that agent
  6. Share the Project with your team via Team workspace → Manage Members
