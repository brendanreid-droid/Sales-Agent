GTM Agent Team Roster & Roles

The Sapia GTM team comprises 8 specialized agents operating in a coordinated workflow. Here is a summary of each agent's role and primary contribution:

  1. 🎯 Sales Director (SAPIA-SD): The orchestrator. Coordinates the weekly send schedule, runs the Monday signal harvests, audits Copywriter drafts against the 8-Point Copywriting Checklist, posts the Slack Morning Digest for approval, and benchmarks Friday performance against real-world metrics.
  2. 🔍 Research Analyst (SAPIA-RA): The scout. Pulls HubSpot newsletter clicks, monitors job transitions, runs Brave Search lookalike scans to discover net-new companies, and flags triggers.
  3. 📊 ICP Scorer (SAPIA-ICP): The qualifier. Automatically scores and tiers prospect accounts (Tier 1/2/3/4) based on a custom 7-dimension rubric to filter out poor-fit leads. Re-scores existing accounts from the target list for consistency.
  4. 🔍 Company Researcher (SAPIA-CR): The profiler. Gathers deep target intel from open-web sources to build 1-page briefs mapping ATS/HRIS technologies, 4-layer pain points, and competitor positioning.
  5. ✍️ Copywriter (SAPIA-CW): The writer. Voice-matches to your reps' profiles and Gong call transcripts, drafting tailored email sequences (Touch 1-4) with high-reply PVP CTAs.
  6. 🏹 Prospect Hunter (SAPIA-PH): The loader. Enriches contacts with verified email and LinkedIn URLs using Lusha (strictly email-only to protect credit limits), checks 90-day cooldowns, and stages records.
  7. 💬 Reply Handler (SAPIA-RH): The closer. Monitors replies natively via SalesLoft, triages intent into 12 buckets, scores BANT criteria, and pushes AE Slack briefs for warm handoffs.
  8. 🧠 GTM Action Thinker (SAPIA-GAT): The strategist. Challenges, brainstorms, and deconstructs campaign ideas or angles, identifying blind spots and setting metrics-backed roadmaps.

Tuesday & Thursday Cadence Schedule

To successfully run outbound cadences twice a week on Tuesdays and Thursdays, the team follows a structured operational rhythm. Below is the weekly timeline and the specific activities required:

[MON: Plan & Score] ➔ [TUE: Outreach 1 (Slack Gate)] ➔ [WED: Deep Research] ➔ [THU: Outreach 2 (Slack Gate)] ➔ [FRI: Audit & Learn]

Monday: Sourcing & Planning (Prep for the Week)

  • Goal: Set the week's accounts and verify targets.
  • Activities:
  • Sales Director harvests new signals (newsletter opens, job changes).
  • Research Analyst processes lookalikes and updates the signals logs.
  • ICP Scorer qualifies all target accounts, keeping only Tier A & B targets.
  • Sales Director builds the Weekly Outreach Plan allocating targets to Tuesday and Thursday batches (cap: 100 unique companies per send day).

Tuesday: Outreach Day 1 (Send Session 1)

  • Goal: Approve and send the first email batch.
  • Activities:
  • 7:30 AM: Copywriter drafts the Tuesday batch emails (run in 4 sessions of 25 emails to avoid token overload).
  • 8:00 AM: Sales Director audits drafts and posts the Morning Digest (up to 100 emails) to Slack.
  • 9:00 AM: Human rep reviews digest in Slack, replying APPROVE or EDIT directly in the thread.
  • 9:30 AM: Prospect Hunter runs Lusha email-only lookup, de-dupes, and stages approved contacts in SalesLoft.
  • 10:00 AM: Sales Director triggers the SalesLoft send sequence.

Wednesday: Deep Profiling (Prep for Thursday)

  • Goal: Build rich briefings for the second send day.
  • Activities:
  • Company Researcher builds deep 1-page briefs for the Thursday targets (mapping 4-layer pain points, ATS tech stack, and competitor positioning).
  • Rep reviews target list for Thursday and updates the vault's objection or case studies folders if needed.

Thursday: Outreach Day 2 (Send Session 2)

  • Goal: Approve and send the second email batch.
  • Activities:
  • 7:30 AM: Copywriter drafts the Thursday batch emails (processed in subsets of 25).
  • 8:00 AM: Sales Director audits drafts and posts the Morning Digest (up to 100 emails) to Slack.
  • 9:00 AM: Human rep reviews and replies APPROVE or EDIT in the Slack thread.
  • 9:30 AM: Prospect Hunter enriches via Lusha and stages approved contacts in SalesLoft.
  • 10:00 AM: Sales Director triggers the SalesLoft send.

Friday: Audit, Learn & Rubric Calibration

  • Goal: Collect performance metrics and feed learnings back to the agents.
  • Activities:
  • Sales Director pulls SalesLoft stats (Sends, Opens, Replies, Bookings) and runs the Outbound Analyst Benchmark check.
  • Sales Director posts the Diagnostic Verdict to #sapia-performance mapping root-cause fixes.
  • Copywriter updates Campaign_Learnings.md with best-performing hooks.
  • ICP Scorer calibrates scoring thresholds based on response signals.

Outbound Email Copywriting Standards

Every outbound email drafted by the Copywriter is audited against a strict, research-backed checklist to ensure it reads like a human-to-human note and maximizes reply rates:

1. The 8-Point Copywriting Checklist

  • Em Dashes (— or –) Ban: Entirely banned. It makes copy read like a marketing brochure, not a personal email.
  • No Rhetorical Hooks: Cliches like "Are you struggling with X?" are banned. Opens directly on verifiable triggers.
  • No Generic Flattery: Empty compliments like "Love what you're building" or "Hope this finds you well" are removed.
  • No Pompous Jargon: Absolute ban on: leverage, synergies, cutting-edge, robust, seamless, empower, streamline, unlock, next-level, paradigm, holistic, best-in-class, world-class.
  • No "I" or "We" Openers: The email body must start with the prospect's context (e.g. Given the Q4 hiring spike at [Company]...).
  • No Meeting Ask in Email 1: Meeting requests are replaced with soft value CTAs.
  • Strict Word Limits: Email 1 is capped at 120 words; follow-up touches are capped at 150 words.
  • Sentence-Case Subject Lines: Capped at 6 words, lowercase, creating curiosity rather than pitching (e.g. frontline turnover at [Company]).

2. Permissionless Value Promise (PVP) CTAs

Generic "book a call" CTA requests are replaced with high-response Permissionless Value Promises (Insight, Benchmark, Playbook, or Diagnostic Questions) that offer standalone value in exchange for a simple "yes" reply: * Insight CTA: "Put together a quick one-pager showing how other retail TA teams automate first-round screening. Worth a send?" * Benchmark CTA: "Retailers like [Competitor] sit at a 12% candidate assessment drop-off rate. Curious how you compare — happy to share the benchmark data?" * Diagnostic Question: "Are you currently using manual resume screening or automated tests first? Reason I ask is that's where candidates usually drop off."

Best Practice Guide: Ongoing Operations

This guide provides operational workflows, copy-paste prompts, and connection steps to run the agent team smoothly.

1. Starting & Resuming Work Sessions

AI agents work best when given clear, contextual triggers to start their run. Copy and paste these exact prompts in your Claude Projects.

A. Starting the Weekly Cycle (Sales Director - Monday)

Paste into SAPIA-[REGION]-SD Project: "Let's kick off our Monday planning session for the [APAC / MENA / UK] region. Sourcing from our signals logs (Newsletter Engagers, Job Movers, Trigger Events), please identify up to 100 unique companies to target this week. Do not exceed 100. Run Lusha lookalike scans via Lusha MCP if we need a pipeline buffer. Once target accounts are identified, generate the Weekly Outreach Plan."

B. Triggering Copywriter Calibration (Copywriter - Tuesday/Thursday)

Paste into SAPIA-[REGION]-CW Project: "We are starting the email drafting session for rep [Your Name]. First, read [Your Name]_Voice.md in 04-Brand_Voice/Sender_Voice_Profiles/ and the winning email examples in 03-Outreach/Email_Winners/ to calibrate. Stop and output your Voice Calibration summary for my approval. Do not draft any emails until I approve this calibration."

C. Continuing After Calibration Approval

Paste into SAPIA-[REGION]-CW Project once calibration is approved: "Calibration approved. Read the target account list for today's batch from our Sales Director's Weekly Outreach Plan. For each account, read the corresponding Company Brief generated by the Company Researcher. Draft the Touch [1/2/3/4] email in accordance with our word count and banned phrase rules. Output the table with the 'Reasoning for angle' column."

D. Resuming a Stuck Session

Paste into any agent Project if a connection fails, a token limit is hit, or the session restarts: "We are resuming our session. Read the latest file status in Sapia-Sales-Vault/ via Filesystem MCP to determine where we left off. Output a brief status summary of completed tasks and tell me what the next step is before running any MCP tools."

2. Setting Up Slack Notifications & Approvals

Reps do not need to check Claude Desktop constantly. The Sales Director and Reply Handler can push digests and alerts directly to your personal Slack DM or a dedicated regional channel, and read your response to continue.

Step 1: Authorize Slack in Claude Desktop

  1. Open Claude Desktop and click the settings cog (or menu) in the bottom-left.
  2. Select Customize (or Connectors).
  3. Find Slack and click Connect / Authorize.
  4. Log into your company's Slack workspace and approve permissions.

Step 2: Retrieve your Channel or User ID

  • For a channel: Open Slack, right-click the channel name (e.g. #sapia-digest-apac), select View channel details, and copy the Channel ID at the bottom (starts with C, e.g., C1234567890).
  • For personal DMs: Click your own profile picture in Slack, select Profile, click the three dots (...), select Copy member ID (starts with U, e.g., U1234567890).

Step 3: Map the ID in the Sales Director Prompt

Open your regional Sales Director prompt file (e.g. agent_prompts/00_sales_director.md) and edit the Slack notifications line under the Territory Configuration block:

- Slack notifications: post to [Insert Channel ID here, e.g. C1234567890] and DM to [Insert Member ID here, e.g. U1234567890]

Step 4: The Slack-First Approval Loop

Once you kick off a session in Claude Desktop: 1. The Sales Director posts the Morning Digest (the 100 drafted emails) to your Slack channel. 2. Review the drafts on your phone or Slack client. 3. Approve directly in Slack: - Reply APPROVE to send the whole batch. - Reply HOLD to pause. - Reply 3 EDIT: Change Woolworths hook to focus on retail growth to revise individual emails. 4. Back in Claude Desktop, simply type:

"Check Slack approvals and run." The agent will read the Slack channel history using the Slack MCP, see your approval or edits, update the drafts, and instruct the Prospect Hunter to stage them in SalesLoft.

3. Active Guardrails & Cost Protections

The agent system is equipped with multiple automated checks to protect your sender reputation, manage Lusha credit costs, and prevent Claude context limits from causing runaway token billing.

Guardrail Name Target Agent Rule / Action Reason
Human-in-the-Loop Gate Sales Director No email auto-sends. Rep must explicitly reply APPROVE via Slack to unlock cadence enrollment. Reputation & quality control
90-Day Outreach Cooldown Prospect Hunter Skip any contact emailed in the last 90 days (checks SalesLoft and Outreach_History_Log.csv). Anti-spam and frequency protection
Unique Company Limit Sales Director Outreach batches restricted to 1 contact per company per batch (strictly different companies). Prevents spamming multiple people at one organization at the same time
Weekly Batch Cap Sales Director Capped at 100 contacts per rep per send day. Excess are rolled over to the next send session. Manageable inbox load & deliverability safety
Score-Before-Enrich Prospect Hunter Never look up a contact via Lusha unless company is qualified as Tier A or B by the ICP Scorer. Credit cost conservation
Email-Only Lusha Lookups Prospect Hunter Strictly configure Lusha query to fetch email addresses only. Scrapes of phone numbers are forbidden. Phone scraping costs 6x extra credits (7 vs 1 credit)
Lusha Weekly Budget Alert Sales Director Alert rep in Slack if projected weekly spend exceeds 200 credits per rep (600 total). Keeps Lusha billing predictable
Token Session Limits All Agents Discovery capped at 50 accounts; briefs at 10 companies; copywriter drafts at 25 emails per session. Avoids context/token overflow
Opt-Out Safety Trigger Reply Handler Immediately remove contact from SalesLoft with zero delay if reply is classified UNSUBSCRIBE or NEGATIVE. Legal compliance & domain reputation
Copywriter Calibration Gate Copywriter Must output calibrated voice profile for human approval before drafting any emails. Guarantees authentic sender voice alignment

4. Daily Hygiene Checklist

  • Check Slack first: Look at #sapia-alerts and #sapia-digest-[region] at 9:00 AM local time on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
  • Keep cadences clean: If a contact replies positively, double-check that the Reply Handler successfully paused their SalesLoft cadence.
  • Empty the spam traps: Review bounces or opt-outs flagged by the Reply Handler once a week to ensure your domain health remains high.
  • Log the wins: When an email books a meeting, copy the body text and paste it into Sapia-Sales-Vault/03-Outreach/Email_Winners/Cold_Winners.md so the Copywriter continually learns from what succeeds.
  • Clear cache weekly: If Claude Desktop runs slowly, close the app and restart to clear the filesystem and MCP connection logs.
  • Lusha Budget Lock: Respect the 200-credit weekly cap. If the Prospect Hunter triggers a warning alert, verify the target list quality before authorizing the override.
