GTM Agent Team Roster & Roles

The Sapia GTM team comprises 8 specialized agents operating in a coordinated workflow. Here is a summary of each agent's role and primary contribution:

  1. 🎯 Sales Director (SAPIA-SD): The orchestrator. Coordinates the weekly send schedule, runs the Monday signal harvests, audits Copywriter drafts against the 8-Point Copywriting Checklist, posts the Slack Morning Digest for approval, and benchmarks Friday performance against real-world metrics.
  2. 🔍 Research Analyst (SAPIA-RA): The scout. Pulls HubSpot newsletter clicks, monitors job transitions, runs Brave Search lookalike scans to discover net-new companies, and flags triggers.
  3. 📊 ICP Scorer (SAPIA-ICP): The qualifier. Automatically scores and tiers prospect accounts (Tier 1/2/3/4) based on a custom 7-dimension rubric to filter out poor-fit leads. Re-scores existing accounts from the target list for consistency.
  4. 🔍 Company Researcher (SAPIA-CR): The profiler. Builds briefs in two formats, Full (all 10 sections: ATS/HRIS, 4-layer pain points, competitor positioning, the works, for a handful of confirmed Tier A accounts) or Lean (5 sections, batched 3-5 companies per dispatch, for the ongoing weekly research cadence). See "Two Types of Company Research" under Wednesday's cadence step for when to use which.
  5. ✍️ Copywriter (SAPIA-CW): The writer. Voice-matches to your reps' profiles and Gong call transcripts, drafting tailored email sequences (Touch 1-4) with high-reply PVP CTAs.
  6. 🏹 Prospect Hunter (SAPIA-PH): The loader. Enriches contacts with verified email and LinkedIn URLs using Lusha (strictly email-only to protect credit limits), checks 90-day cooldowns, and stages records.
  7. 💬 Reply Handler (SAPIA-RH): The closer. Monitors replies natively via SalesLoft, triages intent into 12 buckets, scores BANT criteria, and pushes AE Slack briefs for warm handoffs.
  8. 🧠 GTM Action Thinker (SAPIA-GAT): The strategist. Challenges, brainstorms, and deconstructs campaign ideas or angles, identifying blind spots and setting metrics-backed roadmaps.

Tuesday & Thursday Cadence Schedule

Nothing on this page runs by itself. There is no background job, no scheduler, no cron. Every single line below happens because you, the AE, open Claude Code and type a prompt at that point in the day. The clock times are a suggested rhythm to aim for, not a timer the system keeps on its own, if you don't open Claude Code and prompt it on Monday morning, Monday's planning simply doesn't happen. Each step below now shows the exact prompt to send.

[MON: Plan & Score] ➔ [TUE: Outreach 1 (Slack Gate)] ➔ [WED: Deep Research] ➔ [THU: Outreach 2 (Slack Gate)] ➔ [FRI: Audit & Learn]

Monday: New Research & Planning

Goal: Set the week's accounts and verify targets.

You open Claude Code and send:

"Let's kick off new research. Run any lookalike discovery we need, and get every candidate account through the ICP Scorer, don't use any legacy score from the target list, only your own current score. Then build the week's outreach plan, capped at 20 unique companies per week, 10 per send day, split across Tuesday and Thursday."

This single prompt is what triggers the Sales Director to dispatch Research Analyst (new lookalikes only) and ICP Scorer (qualify every candidate) in sequence, then assemble the Weekly Outreach Plan. Deliberately kept simple and purely about research and outbounding: newsletter engagers, job movers, and Trigger Events log review are all handled separately (see "Twice Monthly" below), not bundled into every run, for now. It won't happen from a shorter prompt like "good morning", be explicit about what you want done.

Tuesday: Outreach Day 1

Goal: Approve and enrol the first email batch. (Sending itself is a separate, final step you do in SalesLoft, see below.)

You open Claude Code and send:

"Draft today's Tuesday batch. Use the accounts from Monday's plan, run the Copywriter in sessions of 25, and post the Morning Digest to Slack once every email passes the 8-point audit."

Once the digest is in Slack, you review and reply APPROVE, HOLD, or an edit note directly in the thread, same as always. Then, back in Claude Code:

"Check Slack for my approval on today's digest and stage the approved contacts in SalesLoft."

That second prompt is what triggers Prospect Hunter to de-dupe, stage, and now also automatically enrol the approved contacts into their SalesLoft cadence, your Slack APPROVE is what authorises that, no extra confirmation needed. Claude doesn't poll Slack on its own, you're telling it when to go check. Enrolling is not sending, no agent ever clicks send, you still open SalesLoft and start the cadence yourself, that's the final gate.

One-time check before you rely on this: in SalesLoft, confirm your cadence's email steps are set to Manual send type, not Automatic. On an Automatic step, SalesLoft sends the instant a contact is enrolled, which would remove your final manual gate entirely. This is a SalesLoft cadence setting, not something Claude Code controls.

Wednesday: Deep Profiling (and New Research, if running it twice a week)

Two different things can happen on a Wednesday, don't confuse them:

1. New Research (optional repeat of Monday), if you want the pipeline topped up mid-week: exactly the same prompt as Monday's "New Research & Planning" above, word for word, same lookalike discovery / ICP scoring step. It's not a different activity that happens to fall on a Wednesday, it's literally Monday's step run again.

2. Deep Profiling (the actual Wednesday-specific step, always runs):

Goal: Build the research briefs for the second send day. There are two different brief formats, pick the right one, see below.

Two Types of Company Research — pick the right one

Using the wrong format either wastes budget on an account that doesn't need it, or under-researches one that does. Confirmed by live testing on 2026-07-15:

Full (Tailored 1:1)Lean (Continuous / Bulk)
Use forA handful of confirmed Tier A accounts entering a real send this week, across all 4 touchesThe standing 20-company/week research cadence, exploratory or discovery-stage accounts

Full-format prompt (a few high-priority accounts, one at a time is fine):

"Build a Full-format brief for Woolworths Group, this is a confirmed Tier A account going into this week's send. Give me the complete 10-section format, snapshot, trigger, tech stack, full org map with buyer, champion and blocker, 4-layer pain points, competitor mapping, leadership stance, the works. Flag anything too thin for genuine personalisation rather than forcing it."

Lean-format prompt (the ongoing weekly cadence, always batched):

"Build Lean-format briefs for these 5 companies in one batch: [Company A], [Company B], [Company C], [Company D], [Company E]. Read the knowledge base once and reuse it across all five, don't re-read it per company. For headcount and a candidate contact, use whatever I've supplied below as Given Firmographics, and don't re-search for anything I've already given you, if I haven't supplied a contact for one of them, state the target title only and mark the name UNKNOWN for Prospect Hunter to resolve. Spend your search budget on finding the single best trigger per company and the pain/opener synthesis only, don't shop around for a better trigger once you've found one that clears the bar."

If you're doing both New Research and Deep Profiling on the same Wednesday, run New Research first, then Deep Profiling (in whichever format fits) on whatever the Weekly Outreach Plan now includes for Thursday.

Thursday: Outreach Day 2

Same two prompts as Tuesday, swap "Tuesday" for "Thursday" in the first one.

Friday: Audit, Learn & Rubric Calibration

Goal: Collect performance metrics and feed learnings back to the agents.

"Pull this week's SalesLoft stats, sends, opens, replies, bookings, run the Outbound Analyst Benchmark check, and post the diagnostic verdict to #brendans-gtm-agent. Then update Campaign_Learnings.md with what worked and what didn't."

Optional: Automate the Monday/Wednesday Research Kickoff

You don't have to open Claude Code and type the research prompt yourself every Monday and Wednesday, Claude Code can schedule a recurring cloud routine that does it for you. This isn't a settings panel to click through, you set it up by asking Claude to do it in a normal chat message.

What it does: runs the "new research" step (Lookalike Discovery, ICP Scorer on every candidate) automatically at a set time, writes the results straight into your vault, then posts a short summary to Slack so you know it's ready. Purely research and outbounding prep, Trigger Events, job movers, and newsletter engagers are deliberately kept out of this, see "Twice Monthly" above.

What it deliberately does not do: draft any emails, post a Morning Digest, enrol anyone in SalesLoft, or send anything. Those all still need you to open Claude Code and give an explicit prompt, per "Nothing on this page runs by itself" above. This routine only feeds the pipeline, it doesn't act on it.

How to set it up

Open a Claude Code session in this repo and send a prompt like:

"Schedule a recurring routine that runs every Monday and Wednesday at 9am Sydney time. It should act as the Sales Director and do the 'new research' step only: run Lookalike Discovery, and get every new or existing candidate through the ICP Scorer, ignoring any legacy score in the target list. Keep this purely to research and outbounding prep, don't touch Trigger Events, job movers, or newsletter engagers, those are handled separately. Do not draft emails, do not post a Morning Digest, do not touch SalesLoft. When it's done, post a short summary to [your Slack channel, e.g. #brendans-gtm-agent or a DM] with how many new lookalikes were found and how many accounts were scored."

Claude Code will confirm the schedule back to you before it's active. A few things worth knowing:

  • This is per-rep. Each rep sets up their own routine pointing at their own vault and territory, there's no shared/central schedule.
  • To check, pause, or change it later, just ask: "List my scheduled tasks" or "Pause the Monday/Wednesday research routine."
  • It still respects every guardrail (territory dedup, legacy-score ban, lookalike write-back), it's running the exact same "new research" step you'd trigger manually, just on a timer instead of a typed prompt.

Outbound Email Copywriting Standards

Every outbound email drafted by the Copywriter is audited against a strict, research-backed checklist to ensure it reads like a human-to-human note and maximizes reply rates:

1. The 8-Point Copywriting Checklist

  • Em Dashes (— or –) Ban: Entirely banned. It makes copy read like a marketing brochure, not a personal email.
  • No Rhetorical Hooks: Cliches like "Are you struggling with X?" are banned. Opens directly on verifiable triggers.
  • No Generic Flattery: Empty compliments like "Love what you're building" or "Hope this finds you well" are removed.
  • No Pompous Jargon: Absolute ban on: leverage, synergies, cutting-edge, robust, seamless, empower, streamline, unlock, next-level, paradigm, holistic, best-in-class, world-class.
  • No "I" or "We" Openers: The email body must start with the prospect's context (e.g. Given the Q4 hiring spike at [Company]...).
  • No Meeting Ask in Email 1: Meeting requests are replaced with soft value CTAs.
  • Strict Word Limits: Email 1 is capped at 135 words; follow-up touches are capped at 150 words.
  • Sentence-Case Subject Lines: Capped at 6 words, lowercase, creating curiosity rather than pitching (e.g. frontline turnover at [Company]).
  • No Cliché CTAs: Never "no strings attached." Never a bare "Worth a look?" as its own sentence, fold it into the offer instead: "Happy to send through [X], if you think it's worth a look?" Check the sender's Voice Profile for their specific preferred close.
  • Sign-off is name only: No company name or title line under the sender's name in the email body, SalesLoft's own signature block handles that. Adding one reads as templated.

2. Permissionless Value Promise (PVP) CTAs

Generic "book a call" CTA requests are replaced with high-response Permissionless Value Promises (Insight, Benchmark, Playbook, or Diagnostic Questions) that offer standalone value in exchange for a simple "yes" reply: * Insight CTA: "Put together a quick one-pager showing how other retail TA teams automate first-round screening. Worth a send?" * Benchmark CTA: "Retailers like [Competitor] sit at a 12% candidate assessment drop-off rate. Curious how you compare — happy to share the benchmark data?" * Diagnostic Question: "Are you currently using manual resume screening or automated tests first? Reason I ask is that's where candidates usually drop off."

Detailed MCP Connection Setup

There are two different ways a service gets connected here, and most reps will only ever need the first one. Check the pre-built connector path first, it's what most people actually use, the manual .mcp.json path is a fallback for whatever doesn't have one yet.

Confirmed 2026-07-15: Slack and Lusha connect as pre-built connectors, no API key, no .mcp.json editing. SalesLoft may need the manual path below, depending on whether a pre-built SalesLoft connector exists on your account when you get to it, check Step A first either way.

Path A — Pre-built connectors (Slack, Lusha, and check for others)

Anthropic hosts and manages these directly. They're connected once, at your claude.ai account level, not inside this repo, and they then work automatically in every Claude Code session and every project you open, no per-repo setup, no keys to copy.

Step 1 — Open claude.ai/customize/connectors

In a browser, go to claude.ai/customize/connectors (log in with the same account you use for Claude Code). You'll see a list of available connectors.

Step 2 — Connect Slack and Lusha

Find each one in the list, click Connect, and follow the on-screen OAuth sign-in, log into your Slack workspace / Lusha account and approve. No key to generate, nothing to paste anywhere.

Step 3 — Check for SalesLoft too

Search for SalesLoft on the same page. If it's listed, connect it exactly the same way, no manual setup needed. If it's not listed, that's when you drop down to Path B below for SalesLoft specifically.

Team / Enterprise plans: connecting a pre-built connector at the account level may require admin permissions. If a connector you need isn't showing up, ask your workspace admin to add it rather than assuming it's broken.

Step 4 — Confirm it's live

Open Claude Code (double-click start.command, or type claude in a terminal opened to this folder) and send:

"Run /mcp and tell me which connectors are live, and which ones are labelled as coming from claude.ai versus set up locally in this project."

Anything connected via Path A shows up labelled "from claude.ai", that's the pre-built, account-level kind. Anything else in that list came from Path B below, set up specifically for this repo.

Path B — Manual setup (for anything without a pre-built connector)

As of 2026-07-15 this means SalesLoft (if it's not listed in Path A when you check), and optionally HubSpot, Gong, and Brave Search, none of which are required to run a first batch. This is the older, per-repo, API-key style of connecting, edit one file, no terminal commands beyond opening the app.

One thing to get right before you start: never paste a real API key into .mcp.json.example or commit it to git. Real keys only ever go in your own .mcp.json (no ".example"), which is gitignored, stays on your machine, and is never shared with the team.

Step 1 — Create your personal credentials file

In Finder (or your file browser), find .mcp.json.example in the repo root. Duplicate it (right-click → Duplicate, or copy/paste), and rename the copy to exactly .mcp.json (drop the ".example"). That's it, no command needed. This new file is already listed in .gitignore, so it will never be committed or shared, it's yours alone. Only add entries here for services you're actually setting up this way, don't duplicate Slack or Lusha into this file, they're already handled by Path A.

Step 3 — Fill in .mcp.json

Open your new .mcp.json in any plain text editor, TextEdit, Notepad, VS Code, whatever's on your machine already. Find each "..." and replace it with the real value you just copied. For example, the SalesLoft block goes from:

"env": { "SALESLOFT_API_KEY": "..." }

to:

"env": { "SALESLOFT_API_KEY": "sk_live_your_actual_key_here" }

Do the same for HubSpot, Gong (two values), Brave Search, and the Filesystem path at the very bottom. Leave any "..." in place for a server you're not using yet, Claude Code just skips it. Save the file when you're done.

Alternative to editing the file by hand: Claude Code can add one of these servers for you from a chat message instead, useful if a service supports OAuth sign-in rather than a static key:

"Add the SalesLoft MCP server, then run /mcp so I can sign in."

That's the claude mcp add command running under the hood, followed by an /mcp browser sign-in, you never have to type the command yourself.

Step 4 — Tell Claude to load it

Back in Claude Code, send this message:

"I've filled in my MCP credentials in .mcp.json. Please confirm which servers connected and test each one."

Claude will run through Filesystem, SalesLoft, HubSpot, Gong, and Brave Search (whichever you've filled in) and tell you plainly which ones are live and which ones need attention, no separate test prompts required on your end.

Troubleshooting

  • A pre-built connector (Slack, Lusha) isn't showing up in Claude Code: check claude.ai/customize/connectors directly, confirm it shows as connected there first. On Team/Enterprise plans, only an admin can add one, ask them rather than trying to work around it with a manual API key.
  • A manually-configured server shows as not connected: double check you saved .mcp.json (not .mcp.json.example) and that the value has no stray quote marks or extra spaces. Ask Claude to re-check after you fix it.
  • SalesLoft key rejected (Path B): confirm your SalesLoft admin has actually enabled the Agentic package first, this is the most common blocker and it's an org setting, not something you can fix on your own key.
  • Filesystem can't find the vault: double-check the path in .mcp.json is the full absolute path (starts with / on Mac) to the Sapia-Sales-Vault folder itself, not its parent folder, and that there are no typos.
  • A connector keeps re-prompting for auth: the OAuth grant may have expired or been revoked on the service's side, just go through the sign-in again, it's harmless to repeat.

Why not Claude Desktop?

Claude Desktop is a genuinely easier product if someone truly cannot use a terminal at all, its Connectors panel is point-and-click with zero typing. But it can't run this system as built: there's no equivalent to .claude/agents/ or CLAUDE.md, so there's no orchestrator automatically dispatching the 8 specialists in parallel, that has to be done by hand, pasting between separate Projects one at a time. It's not the same stack running on a different app, it's a materially more manual process. For a team that needs to work consistently, that outweighs the one-click terminal step Claude Code asks for via start.command. If you still need the Desktop path for a specific person, claude_desktop_config_template.json is in the repo and uses the same API keys from the table above, just pasted into that file instead and placed at ~/Library/Application Support/Claude/claude_desktop_config.json (Mac) or %APPDATA%\Claude\claude_desktop_config.json (Windows), then restart Claude Desktop.

Best Practice Guide: Ongoing Operations

This guide provides operational workflows and copy-paste prompts to run the agent team smoothly. One thing to be clear on before any of this: you talk to a single Claude Code session, one conversation, one "Sales Director." There's no separate Project per agent to paste into anymore (that was the old Claude Desktop model), the Sales Director reads its identity from CLAUDE.md automatically and dispatches the right specialist itself when your prompt calls for it. Your job is to tell it what outcome you want, clearly enough that it knows which agents to involve.

1. Core Prompts You'll Actually Use

These are the same trigger prompts from the Tuesday & Thursday Cadence Schedule above, collected in one place for quick reference, plus the two lower-priority signal workflows.

A. Monday Planning

"Let's kick off new research. Run any lookalike discovery we need, and get every candidate account through the ICP Scorer, don't use any legacy score from the target list, only your own current score. Then build the week's outreach plan, capped at 20 unique companies per week, 10 per send day, split across Tuesday and Thursday."

B. Drafting a Send-Day Batch

"Draft today's [Tuesday/Thursday] batch. Use the accounts from Monday's plan, run the Copywriter in sessions of 25, and post the Morning Digest to Slack once every email passes the 8-point audit."

C. Checking Approvals & Enrolling in SalesLoft

"Check Slack for my approval on today's digest and stage the approved contacts in SalesLoft."

This enrols approved contacts into their cadence automatically, it does not send anything. You still open SalesLoft and start the cadence yourself when you're ready, that's the final manual step.

D. Twice Monthly: Signal Review, Job Movers & Newsletter Engagers (Lower Priority)

Deliberately separate from the Monday/Wednesday research routine, run this on its own roughly every 1-2 weeks:

"Run a HubSpot newsletter engagement pull. Only flag contacts whose company is in my own Target_Account_List.csv, exclude anything outside my territory, and route any ICP-matched engager to the Warm Engagement workflow."

"I've dropped this month's LinkedIn Sales Nav export at [file path]. Process it for Champion Movers, flag anyone who's moved from a Sapia customer or existing prospect to a new company, and queue new ICP-matched contacts for enrichment."

Anything flagged here rolls into the next Monday/Wednesday's Account Prioritisation step automatically, no need to run it as its own send day.

E. Resuming a Stuck Session

"We got interrupted. Read the latest file status in Sapia-Sales-Vault/ to work out where we left off, give me a brief status summary, and tell me what the next step is before you run any tools."

General rule for all of these: be explicit about the outcome and any constraint that matters (a cap, a cadence, a rule to enforce), a vague prompt like "let's do Monday stuff" won't reliably trigger the full workflow, the Sales Director isn't guessing your intent, it's following what you actually asked for.

2. Setting Up Slack Notifications & Approvals

Reps do not need to check Claude Code constantly. The Sales Director and Reply Handler can push digests and alerts directly to your personal Slack DM or a dedicated channel, and read your response to continue.

Step 1: Authorize Slack

Covered in "Detailed MCP Connection Setup → Path A" above, Slack connects as a pre-built connector at claude.ai/customize/connectors, a one-time OAuth sign-in at the account level. Nothing further to do here once that's done, and nothing to configure inside this repo.

Step 2: Retrieve your Channel or User ID

  • For a channel: Open Slack, right-click your private channel name (e.g. #brendans-gtm-agent, or #[firstname]-gtm-agent for your own), select View channel details, and copy the Channel ID at the bottom (starts with C, e.g., C1234567890).
  • For personal DMs: Click your own profile picture in Slack, select Profile, click the three dots (...), select Copy member ID (starts with U, e.g., U1234567890).

Step 3: Tell the Sales Director where to post

No file editing needed for this one, just tell Claude in chat, e.g. "Post the Morning Digest to channel C1234567890 and DM me at U1234567890 as well." It'll use that going forward for the session.

Step 4: The Slack-First Approval Loop

Once you kick off a session in Claude Code:

  1. The Sales Director posts the Morning Digest (the drafted emails) to your Slack channel.
  2. Review the drafts on your phone or Slack client.
  3. Approve directly in Slack:
    • Reply APPROVE to send the whole batch.
    • Reply HOLD to pause.
    • Reply 3 EDIT: Change Woolworths hook to focus on retail growth to revise individual emails.
  4. Back in Claude Code, simply type:

    "Check Slack approvals and run."
    The agent will read the Slack channel history via the Slack MCP, see your approval or edits, update the drafts, and instruct the Prospect Hunter to stage them in SalesLoft.

4. Daily Hygiene Checklist

  • Check Slack first: Look at #brendans-gtm-agent (or your own rep's equivalent private channel) at 9:00 AM local time on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
  • Keep cadences clean: If a contact replies positively, double-check that the Reply Handler successfully paused their SalesLoft cadence.

  • Log the wins: When an email books a meeting, copy the body text and paste it into Sapia-Sales-Vault/03-Outreach/Email_Winners/Cold_Winners.md so the Copywriter continually learns from what succeeds.
  • Restart weekly: If Claude Code feels slow or an MCP connector seems stuck, close the session and relaunch (double-click start.command again) to clear connection state.
  • Lusha Budget Lock: Respect the 200-credit weekly cap. If the Prospect Hunter triggers a warning alert, verify the target list quality before authorizing the override.
